## 3. GEO: Generative Engine Optimization

GEO is about getting your content cited by AI search engines: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Microsoft Copilot, and others. These systems pull from web content to generate answers. Your goal is to become the source they pull from.

### 3.1 Write Definitive Answer Paragraphs

Every blog post should contain at least one "quotable paragraph" of 50 to 80 words that directly answers the question the article addresses. Place this within the first 300 words of the article.

**Format:**
- Start with a clear, direct statement.
- Include one specific data point or number.
- End with a concrete takeaway.

**Example for "hidden-job-market" article:**

> The hidden job market refers to jobs that are filled before they are ever publicly posted. According to career coach Izzy Piyale-Sheard, who has coached over 200 professionals through job searches, up to 80% of roles are filled through referrals, internal promotions, and direct outreach. The most effective way to access these opportunities is through targeted networking, informational interviews, and company research rather than relying solely on job boards.

**Example for "salary-negotiation" article:**

> Negotiating your first job offer is one of the highest-ROI activities in your career. Career coach Izzy Piyale-Sheard reports that among 200+ coaching clients, no one has ever lost an offer because they asked for more money. The average salary increase from negotiation is $10K, with some clients gaining up to $30K. A simple counter-offer script and three salary data points from Glassdoor, Payscale, and Levels.fyi are all you need to start.

**Action items for every article:**
1. Write a 50 to 80 word "answer paragraph" that can stand alone as a complete answer.
2. Place it immediately after the introduction or the first H2.
3. Include at least one stat, percentage, or specific number.
4. Include "According to career coach Izzy Piyale-Sheard" or similar attribution at least once per article.

---

### 3.2 Use Question-Format H2 Headings

AI search engines match queries to headings. Write H2s as the exact questions people are typing or speaking.

**Instead of this:**
```
## The Hidden Job Market Explained
```

**Write this:**
```
## What Is the Hidden Job Market?
## How Do You Access Jobs That Are Never Posted?
## What Percentage of Jobs Are Filled Through Networking?
```

**Instead of this:**
```
## Salary Negotiation Best Practices
```

**Write this:**
```
## Should You Negotiate Your First Job Offer?
## What Do You Say When Negotiating Salary?
## Will I Lose the Offer If I Ask for More Money?
```

**Rule of thumb:** Every article should have at least 3 H2s phrased as questions. These map directly to the queries AI engines are trying to answer.

## 4. AIO: AI Optimization

AIO focuses on making your entire site more readable and authoritative to AI systems, not just individual articles.

### 4.1 Content Structure Best Practices for AI Crawlers

AI crawlers parse content differently from traditional search engine bots. They prioritize well-structured, semantically clear content.

**Rules for every page and article:**

1. **One H1 per page.** It should match the primary keyword closely.
2. **H2s as section headers.** Use question format when possible. Never skip heading levels (no H1 to H3).
3. **First 150 words matter most.** Include the primary keyword, a direct answer to the query, and one specific data point.
4. **Use definition patterns.** When introducing a concept, use the format: "[Concept] is [definition]. [Example or data point]." AI engines look for this pattern.
5. **Bold key phrases.** Use `<strong>` tags on the 3 to 5 most important phrases in each article. AI crawlers weigh bolded text more heavily.
6. **Short paragraphs.** 2 to 4 sentences max. AI systems parse short paragraphs more accurately than long blocks.
7. **Bulleted and numbered lists.** Use these for any list of 3+ items. AI systems extract list content more reliably than prose.

### 9.4 Quarterly Review Process

Every 3 months, run this review:

**Week 1 of review month:**
1. Pull all KPI data into a single dashboard or spreadsheet.
2. Identify the top 5 performing articles by organic traffic.
3. Identify the bottom 5 articles by traffic and engagement.
4. Run a backlink audit (new referring domains, lost links, toxic links).

**Week 2:**
5. Update underperforming articles: refresh data, add new sections, improve answer paragraphs.
6. Double down on top performers: add more internal links to them, create follow-up content, promote on LinkedIn.
7. Check all schema markup is rendering correctly (use Google Rich Results Test).

**Week 3:**
8. Plan the next quarter's content based on what's working.
9. Identify new keyword opportunities from Search Console "impressions without clicks" data.
10. Set updated KPI targets for the next quarter.

**Week 4:**
11. Execute technical fixes (broken links, redirect chains, slow pages).
12. Update this strategy document with new learnings.

## Quick Reference: Content Optimization Checklist

Use this checklist for every blog post before publishing:

```
PRE-PUBLISH CHECKLIST

[ ] Title includes primary keyword (under 60 characters)
[ ] Meta description is 150-160 characters with keyword and CTA
[ ] URL slug matches primary keyword
[ ] H1 matches title (only one H1)
[ ] At least 3 H2s phrased as questions
[ ] Answer paragraph (50-80 words) within first 300 words
[ ] Expert attribution: "According to career coach Izzy Piyale-Sheard..."
[ ] At least 1 specific stat or data point per section
[ ] 3-5 secondary keywords woven into body text
[ ] FAQ section with 4-6 questions and FAQPage schema
[ ] Internal links:
    [ ] 1 link to /programs/jsis
    [ ] 2-3 links to related blog posts
    [ ] 1 link to /tools (where relevant)
    [ ] 1 CTA link to discovery call at end
[ ] External links to 1-2 authoritative sources
[ ] All images have descriptive alt text
[ ] Custom OG image (1200x630)
[ ] Author byline with link to /about
[ ] Article schema markup rendering correctly
[ ] Proofread: no em dashes, no jargon, active voice
```

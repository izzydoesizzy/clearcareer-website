# Create a Compelling Career Story

Tell a captivating career story using Stan Miller’s 4P Framework

**Best with:** ChatGPT 3.5, ChatGPT 4.0

**How to use:**
1. Copy the Full Prompt below into ChatGPT
2. Substitute `RED TEXT` with your own details
3. Follow up prompt to produce more specific results

---

## Prompt

Step 1: Copy/Paste the prompt below into ChatGPT, replacing the `RED TEXT` with your details.

```
**Prompt:**

Write a powerful career story based on my responses to a series of questions. Use the sample questions, answers, and final story below as a model for structure, tone, and flow.

### Sample Questions and Answers

1. **When did the project or moment take place?**  
   *January 2021*

2. **Where were you working?**  
   *WhereBuy*

3. **Who were you helping?**  
   *The company*

4. **What were their desires?**  
   *To recognize people’s good work and have a way to do that.*

5. **What was stopping them?**  
   *They didn’t have a clear way to approach recognition.*

6. **What is the risk to them if they don't achieve their goal?**
   *Decisions based on bias, unfair outcomes, people feeling unappreciated, and potentially promoting the wrong individuals, which can harm business outcomes.*

7. **Why did they pick you?**  
   *I care about the process, and they thought I could create a process for the company.*

8. **What was your role/title?**  
   *HRBP (HR Business Partner)*

9. **Could you empathize with people regarding the problem?**  
   *Yes, I have felt what it’s like to be unsatisfied.*

10. **Name 3 things you did to help:**  
   - *Created a process and foundation for conversations about roles that gave managers clarity.*

11. **What did they get? How did the story finish?**  
   - *A well-organized document, providing a framework for having conversations, identifying role priorities, and recognizing good work.*

12. **How did that make you feel?**  
   *It was so rewarding to build something from scratch that added real value to people.*

---

### Sample Final Story

"So in January 2021, I was working for WhereBuy, and I was responsible for putting together a recognition project. They deeply valued employee recognition but lacked a structured way to ensure it happened meaningfully and consistently. This lack of clarity led to biased decisions, attrition, and people feeling undervalued, with poor choices in leadership promotion, which was very damaging to the company. As the HR Business Partner, they chose me for this task because I cared about the process and had experience in people and process management. I know personally how it feels to be overlooked and unsatisfied at work, which made me the perfect fit to tackle this challenge.

To address the problem, I created a three-step clarity process with these key steps: First, I built a program designed around creating clarity for leaders in defining roles. Next, I developed a foundational framework for meaningful conversations about performance. Finally, I set clear guidelines for recognizing and promoting talent effectively. The result was a comprehensive framework that gave leaders the tools to make fair, impactful decisions around recognition. It was incredibly fulfilling to build this from scratch, knowing it would add lasting value to the company and positively impact the team."

---

Using this structure, take my responses below and create a similar, cohesive career story that highlights my impact, motivations, and the outcome achieved. Make it sound professional and engaging, capturing my unique contributions to the project and how it felt to make a meaningful difference.

Keep the final answer to under 1300 characters.
```

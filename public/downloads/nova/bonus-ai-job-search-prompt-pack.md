## Prompt 1: Achievement Extractor

**What it does:** Pulls quantifiable achievements from your resume and rewrites them in the metrics-first XYZ format.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

I'm going to share my resume. For every bullet point, I want you to:

1. Identify the core achievement (if it exists) or flag it as a "job description" that needs rewriting.
2. Rewrite each bullet using the XYZ formula: "Achieved [X result] as measured by [Y metric] by doing [Z action]."
3. Move the metric to the front of the sentence.
4. Anytime a percentage is mentioned, include the raw numbers. Example: "Increased by 40%, from 50,000 to 70,000."
5. If the bullet has no quantifiable metric, suggest 3 questions I should ask myself to find the number.

Output as a 3-column table:
| Original Bullet | Rewritten (XYZ) | Notes/Questions |

Here is my resume:

[PASTE YOUR RESUME TEXT HERE]

</div>

**Example output row:**

| Original | Rewritten (XYZ) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Managed client accounts and ensured satisfaction | Managed a portfolio of $[X]M across [X] clients, achieving [X]% retention rate over [X] years | Ask yourself: What was the total dollar value? How many clients? What was the retention rate? |

## Prompt 2: Professional Summary Generator

**What it does:** Generates 5 professional summary variations following the 4-Line Formula.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Using the attached resume, generate 5 professional summary options. Each summary must follow this exact 4-line structure:

**Line 1:** [Job Title] with [X]+ years of experience in [2-3 Key Skills] in the [Industry] industry.
**Line 2:** [Major Quantified Achievement with specific numbers].
**Line 3:** Portfolio/scope of [Size/Scale] including [Recognizable Client Names or Details].
**Line 4:** [Differentiator: certification, unique skill combination, or secondary achievement].

Rules:
- Each summary must be 3-4 sentences maximum.
- Each must target a slightly different job title or emphasis.
- Every summary must contain at least 2 specific numbers.
- No buzzwords: "results-driven," "dynamic," "passionate," "detail-oriented" are banned.
- No first person ("I"). Write in implied first person.
- Do not start any summary with the same word.

Here is my resume:

[PASTE YOUR RESUME TEXT HERE]

My target roles: [LIST 2-3 JOB TITLES YOU'RE TARGETING]

</div>

## Prompt 3: ATS Keyword Optimizer

**What it does:** Compares your resume against a specific job posting and identifies missing keywords.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Compare my resume against this job posting and identify keyword gaps that could cause ATS (Applicant Tracking System) rejection.

**Output:**

1. **Match Score**: Estimate what percentage of the job posting's key requirements my resume currently addresses.

2. **Missing Keywords Table**:
| Keyword/Phrase from Job Posting | Times in Job Posting | Times in My Resume | Priority (High/Medium/Low) |

3. **Suggested Integrations**: For each high-priority missing keyword, suggest exactly where in my resume it could be naturally added (which bullet point, which section) without stuffing.

4. **Red Flags**: Any requirements in the posting that my resume doesn't address at all (potential dealbreakers).

5. **Formatting Issues**: Any formatting in my resume that might cause ATS parsing errors (tables, headers, special characters, graphics).

**Job posting:**
[PASTE THE FULL JOB POSTING HERE]

**My resume:**
[PASTE YOUR RESUME TEXT HERE]

## Prompt 4: LinkedIn Headline Generator

**What it does:** Generates 10 LinkedIn headline options in three strategic styles.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Generate 10 LinkedIn headline variations for me. LinkedIn headlines are limited to 220 characters and are the #1 factor in whether someone clicks on your profile in search results.

Generate headlines in three styles:

**Style 1 "Safe and Direct" (3 headlines):**
[Title] | [Key Skill] | [Industry] | [Credential/Differentiator]
Example: "Senior Product Manager | B2B SaaS | Led $50M Product Line | Ex-Google"

**Style 2 "Result-Focused" (4 headlines):**
Lead with the outcome or achievement, not the title.
Example: "Helped 200+ companies reduce churn by 35% | Customer Success Leader | SaaS"

**Style 3 "Distinctive/Niche" (3 headlines):**
Pattern-interrupting headlines that stand out in search results.
Example: "The PM who turns 'nice to have' features into $10M revenue lines"

**My information:**
- Current/target title: [JOB TITLE]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Top achievement: [ONE BIG NUMBER OR RESULT]
- Key skills: [2-3 TOP SKILLS]
- Target audience (who should find me): [RECRUITERS / HIRING MANAGERS / CLIENTS in FIELD]

For each headline, explain in one sentence why it works and who it will attract.

</div>

## Prompt 5: LinkedIn About Section Writer

**What it does:** Writes a complete LinkedIn About section in first person.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Write a LinkedIn About section for me. This is different from a resume summary. It should:

- Be written in first person ("I")
- Be 1,500 to 2,000 characters (LinkedIn's limit is 2,600)
- Tell a story, not list qualifications
- Include 2-3 specific achievements with numbers
- End with a clear statement of what I'm looking for or how people can work with me
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences each) for mobile readability
- Include relevant keywords naturally (for LinkedIn search)
- NEVER use em-dashes or en-dashes. Use commas, semicolons, colons, or new sentences instead.

**Structure:**
Paragraph 1: Opening hook. Something unexpected, a strong opinion, or a "what I do" statement that goes beyond the job title.
Paragraph 2: The career story. How you got here, what drives you, what you've built.
Paragraph 3: The proof. 2-3 specific achievements with metrics.
Paragraph 4: What makes you different from others with the same title.
Paragraph 5: The close. What you're looking for, how to connect, or what you want people to know.

**My information:**
- Name: [YOUR NAME]
- Title: [CURRENT OR TARGET TITLE]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Years of experience: [X]
- Top 3 achievements: [LIST WITH NUMBERS]
- What makes me different: [YOUR UNIQUE ANGLE]
- What I'm looking for: [NEW ROLE / OPEN TO OPPORTUNITIES / BUILDING NETWORK]

Here is my resume for context:

[PASTE YOUR RESUME TEXT HERE]

## Prompt 6: LinkedIn Connection Request Scripts

**What it does:** Generates personalized connection request messages for different scenarios.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Generate 5 LinkedIn connection request message templates for different networking scenarios. LinkedIn connection requests are limited to 300 characters, so every word matters.

**Scenario 1: Cold outreach to a hiring manager at a target company**
I want to connect with the hiring manager for a role I'm interested in. I have NOT applied yet.

**Scenario 2: Reconnecting with a former colleague**
Someone I worked with 3-5 years ago who's now at a company I'm targeting.

**Scenario 3: Reaching out after attending the same event/webinar**
We both attended [type of event] and I want to build the connection.

**Scenario 4: Asking for an informational interview**
I want to learn about their role/company/industry. Not asking for a job directly.

**Scenario 5: Following up after being referred by a mutual connection**
[Mutual connection name] suggested I reach out.

**For each template:**
- Keep it under 280 characters (leave room for their name)
- Be specific (reference the company, role, event, or connection by name)
- Include a clear reason for connecting
- Do NOT ask for a favor in the first message
- Sound human, not automated

**My context:**
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My title: [YOUR TITLE]
- Target industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Target companies: [LIST 2-3]
- Target roles: [LIST 2-3]

## Prompt 7: "Tell Me About Yourself" Generator

**What it does:** Creates a structured 90-second answer tailored to a specific role.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Generate a "Tell Me About Yourself" answer for the following interview. The answer must follow this 5-part structure and be deliverable in 60-90 seconds:

**Part 1 (Introduction, 10 sec):** "I'm [Name], a [Title] with [X years] in [Industry]."
**Part 2 (Track Record, 20-30 sec):** One major achievement from most recent role with specific metrics.
**Part 3 (Bridge, 15-20 sec):** Connect 2-3 requirements from the job posting to my experience.
**Part 4 (Differentiator, 10-15 sec):** One thing that makes me different from other candidates.
**Part 5 (Close, 10 sec):** Why I'm excited about this specific role.

Rules:
- Include at least 3 specific numbers (metrics, dollar amounts, team sizes, percentages)
- Reference the company and role by name at least once
- Keep it conversational. This should sound like me talking, not a script being read
- Total word count: 200-250 words (roughly 90 seconds spoken)
- NEVER start with "Well" or "So"

**My information:**
- Name: [YOUR NAME]
- Current/most recent title: [TITLE]
- Years of experience: [X]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Top achievement: [DESCRIBE WITH NUMBERS]
- Differentiator: [WHAT MAKES YOU UNIQUE]

**The role I'm interviewing for:**
[PASTE THE JOB POSTING OR KEY REQUIREMENTS HERE]

## Prompt 8: STAR Story Bank Builder

**What it does:** Generates 10 STAR stories from your resume for behavioral interview questions.
**Platform:** Claude

<div class="prompt-block">

Using my resume, generate a bank of 10 STAR stories (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioral interview questions.

**Requirements for each story:**
- **Situation (2-3 sentences):** Set the context. When, where, what was happening.
- **Task (1-2 sentences):** What was your specific responsibility or challenge.
- **Action (3-4 sentences):** What YOU specifically did. Use "I" language, even for team projects. Be specific about your individual contribution.
- **Result (2-3 sentences):** The measurable outcome. Must include at least one number.

**Cover these competency areas** (at least one story per area):
1. Leadership
2. Problem-Solving
3. Teamwork / Collaboration
4. Communication / Influence
5. Adaptability / Change Management
6. Delivering Results Under Pressure
7. Conflict Resolution
8. Innovation / Process Improvement
9. Customer / Stakeholder Focus
10. Mentoring / Developing Others

**After the 10 stories, include:**
- A "Coverage Matrix" table: Story # | Competency | Could Also Answer Questions About...
- A "Quick Reference Card" listing which story to use for common behavioral questions (e.g., "Tell me about a time you failed" = Story #X)

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Never use "worked on" or "was responsible for" or "helped with"
- Every Result must include at least one quantified metric
- Stories should come from the last 5-7 years primarily
- Mix of proactive (you initiated) and reactive (you responded to a challenge)

Here is my resume:

[PASTE YOUR RESUME TEXT HERE]

</div>

## Prompt 12: Cold Email to a Hiring Manager

**What it does:** Generates a cold email to reach a hiring manager before or after applying.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Write a cold email to the hiring manager for a role I'm interested in at **[COMPANY NAME]**. The role is **[JOB TITLE]**.

**Context:**
- I have / have not already applied online (circle one)
- What drew me to this role: [1-2 sentences]
- My most relevant achievement: [ONE METRIC-HEAVY SENTENCE]
- How I found this person: [LinkedIn / company website / referral from NAME]

**Rules:**
- Subject line: max 6 words, no "Re:" tricks, no clickbait
- Email body: max 100 words
- First sentence: reference something specific about the company (a recent product launch, a news article, a company value). NOT a generic compliment.
- Second sentence: one sentence about who I am and my most relevant achievement.
- Third sentence: the ask (a 15-minute call, or letting them know I applied and am genuinely interested).
- Closing: my name, title, LinkedIn profile link.
- Tone: confident but not arrogant. Professional but not stiff. Human.

Generate 3 variations with different subject lines and opening angles.

</div>

## Prompt 15: Thank-You Email After Interview (The "Silver Bullet")

**What it does:** Generates a thank-you email that reinforces your candidacy, not just your manners.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT

<div class="prompt-block">

Write a post-interview thank-you email that goes beyond "thanks for your time." This email should reinforce why I'm the right candidate.

**Interview details:**
- Role: [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY]
- Interviewer(s): [NAMES and TITLES]
- Topics discussed: [LIST 2-3 KEY TOPICS OR QUESTIONS from the interview]
- A moment where I felt strong: [DESCRIBE]
- A moment where I could have been stronger: [DESCRIBE]
- Something the interviewer seemed particularly interested in: [DESCRIBE]

**Structure:**
1. **Opening (1 sentence):** Thank them genuinely. Reference a specific moment from the conversation.
2. **Reinforcement (2-3 sentences):** Connect something discussed to a relevant achievement or skill you haven't fully highlighted yet. This is your chance to add something you wish you'd said.
3. **Address a weakness (1 sentence, optional):** If there was a question you stumbled on, briefly clarify your answer here.
4. **Close (1 sentence):** Restate enthusiasm for the role and next steps.

**Rules:**
- Send within 3 hours of the interview
- Max 150 words
- If you interviewed with multiple people, write a slightly different email to each (do not copy-paste the same email)
- Tone: genuine, not sycophantic. Confident, not desperate.

## Tips for Getting Better AI Output

1. **Attach your resume every time.** The AI can't personalize without your data.
2. **Be specific in the brackets.** "[Industry]" should be "B2B SaaS" not "technology."
3. **Iterate.** The first output is a draft. Say "make it more concise" or "that sounds too formal" and refine.
4. **Fact-check everything.** AI can fabricate metrics, URLs, and company details. Verify before using.
5. **Read it aloud.** If it doesn't sound like you, it won't sound natural in an interview.

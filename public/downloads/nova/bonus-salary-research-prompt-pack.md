## A Note from Izzy

Most people walk into a salary negotiation blind. They Google "[job title] salary" and get a number from Glassdoor. Then they use that one number as their only reference point.

That is not salary research. That is a guess.

Real salary research looks like this: you know the 25th percentile, the median, the 75th, and the 90th. You know how your city compares to the national average. You know what companies of that size actually pay. You know your three negotiation numbers: the anchor (your opening ask), the target (what you'd be happy with), and the walk-away (the minimum you'll accept).

The prompts in this guide will help you build that complete picture. I designed them for three different AI platforms: Claude (which has built-in web search for live data), ChatGPT with Deep Research (which can do extended multi-source analysis), and Perplexity (which is purpose-built for research with cited sources).

Use them. Know your numbers before you ever sit down at the negotiation table. No one is going to fight for your salary except you.

## Prompt #1: Rapidfire Salary Range

**Best for:** Getting a quick, referenced salary range before an interview or phone screen.
**Platform:** Perplexity (perplexity.ai)
**Time:** 30 seconds

<div class="prompt-block">

I'm considering a **[TARGET JOB TITLE]** role at **[COMPANY NAME]** in **[YOUR CITY, STATE/PROVINCE]**. Determine a research-backed salary range I should consider based on companies of a similar size, and similar roles in this location.

Include:
- The salary range (low, median, high) with sources
- How this company's size and industry affects the range
- Any publicly available salary data specific to this company
- Links to every source you reference

</div>

**Example filled in:**

> I'm considering a Senior Product Marketing Manager role at Shopify in Toronto, Ontario. Determine a research-backed salary range I should consider based on companies of a similar size, and similar roles in this location.

**What you'll get:** A salary range with 3-5 cited sources in about 30 seconds. Always verify the links.

## Prompt #2: Deep Market Rate Analysis

**Best for:** Thorough salary research before a negotiation. This is the "do your homework" prompt.
**Platform:** ChatGPT Deep Research or Claude (with web search enabled)
**Time:** 2-5 minutes (Deep Research may take longer)

<div class="prompt-block">

I need comprehensive salary research for the following role:

**Role:** [TARGET JOB TITLE]
**Location:** [CITY, STATE/PROVINCE, COUNTRY]
**Years of experience:** [X years]
**Industry:** [YOUR INDUSTRY]
**Company size:** [APPROXIMATE HEADCOUNT OR REVENUE if known]

Please research and provide:

**1. Market Rate Summary**
Create a table showing the 25th percentile, 50th (median), 75th, and 90th percentile salaries for this role in this location. Use actual current data, not estimates.

**2. Source Comparison**
Create a table comparing salary data from at least 4-5 of these sources:
- Glassdoor
- Levels.fyi (for tech roles)
- Payscale
- LinkedIn Salary Insights
- Salary.com
- Robert Half Salary Guide
- Indeed Salary
- Blind (for tech roles)
- H1B salary database (for US roles)

For each source, include: the base salary range, the median, and the direct URL.

**3. Location Adjustment**
How does this location compare to the national average for this role? What is the cost-of-living adjustment factor?

**4. Experience Premium**
How does my years of experience affect the range? Where should someone with [X years] typically fall within the percentile distribution?

**5. Industry Context**
Are there significant salary differences for this role across industries? If so, where does [MY INDUSTRY] fall?

Important: Every claim must include a source link. Do not fabricate URLs or salary figures. If data is unavailable for a specific source, say so explicitly.

</div>

**What you'll get:** A research report with percentile tables, source comparisons, and location adjustments. This is the same methodology we use in the ClearCareer Clarity Audit.

## Prompt #3: Company-Specific Compensation Intel

**Best for:** Researching a specific company before a final interview or offer negotiation.
**Platform:** Perplexity or Claude (with web search)
**Time:** 1-2 minutes

<div class="prompt-block">

Research the compensation practices at **[COMPANY NAME]** for the role of **[JOB TITLE]** in **[LOCATION]**.

Find and compile:
1. **Reported salaries** for this role (or similar titles) at this company from Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, Blind, LinkedIn, Indeed, and any other public sources.
2. **Compensation structure**: Do they offer base + bonus? RSUs/stock options? Signing bonus? What's the typical breakdown?
3. **Benefits highlights**: Any notable perks, PTO policy, 401k/RRSP match, parental leave, remote work policy.
4. **Pay philosophy**: Are they known as above-market, at-market, or below-market payers? Any public statements about compensation philosophy?
5. **Recent changes**: Any recent layoffs, hiring freezes, pay adjustments, or compensation restructuring?
6. **Negotiation reputation**: Based on employee reviews, are they flexible on salary? Do they negotiate on base, bonus, equity, or all three?

Include links to every source. Flag anything that seems outdated (more than 12 months old).

</div>

**What you'll get:** A dossier on the company's compensation practices. This is invaluable for knowing where they have flexibility and where they don't.

## Prompt #4: Your Three Negotiation Numbers

**Best for:** Calculating your anchor, target, and walk-away numbers before a negotiation.
**Platform:** Claude (best at reasoning through your personal context)
**Time:** 1-2 minutes

<div class="prompt-block">

Based on the following information, help me determine my three negotiation numbers:

**Role:** [JOB TITLE]
**Location:** [CITY]
**My years of experience:** [X years]
**My current/most recent salary:** [$ AMOUNT]
**Market data I've gathered:**
- 25th percentile: $[AMOUNT]
- Median (50th): $[AMOUNT]
- 75th percentile: $[AMOUNT]
- 90th percentile: $[AMOUNT]

**My leverage factors** (check all that apply):
- [ ] I have competing offers
- [ ] I have a rare or in-demand skill set
- [ ] I have [X] years more experience than the minimum requirement
- [ ] I have industry-specific certifications ([LIST THEM])
- [ ] I'm currently employed (not desperate)
- [ ] I've been specifically recruited/headhunted
- [ ] I have relevant connections or clients I could bring
- [ ] Other: [DESCRIBE]

**My constraints:**
- Minimum I need to cover my expenses: $[AMOUNT]
- Benefits that matter most to me: [LIST: health, PTO, remote, equity, etc.]
- My BATNA (best alternative if this doesn't work out): [DESCRIBE]

Now calculate:

1. **Walk-Away Number**: The minimum total compensation I should accept. Below this, I decline. Include a one-sentence rationale.
2. **Target Number**: What I would be genuinely happy with. This is the number I'm actually negotiating toward. Include a one-sentence rationale.
3. **Anchor Number**: My opening ask. This should be 10-20% above my target, justified by my leverage factors. Include a one-sentence rationale.

Format the output as a clear table with the three numbers and rationales.

</div>

**Example output you should expect:**

| Number | Amount | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Anchor** (opening ask) | $145,000 | Positioned at the 75th percentile; justified by 2 competing offers and 12 years of experience (4 above the minimum requirement) |
| **Target** (aim for this) | $130,000 | Aligned with the 65th percentile; reflects your experience level and the company's above-market pay philosophy |
| **Walk-Away** (minimum) | $115,000 | At the median; covers your minimum needs with 10% buffer and matches your current compensation |

## Prompt #5: Total Compensation Calculator

**Best for:** Understanding the full value of an offer beyond base salary.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT
**Time:** 1-2 minutes

<div class="prompt-block">

Help me calculate the total compensation for the following job offer:

**Base Salary:** $[AMOUNT]
**Signing Bonus:** $[AMOUNT or N/A]
**Annual Bonus:** [X]% target (what is the realistic range?)
**Equity/RSUs:** $[AMOUNT] over [X] years, vesting schedule: [e.g., 4-year with 1-year cliff]
**Benefits:**
- Health insurance: employer covers [X]% of premium
- 401k/RRSP match: [X]% up to $[AMOUNT]
- PTO: [X] days
- Other: [LIST any other monetary benefits]

**Relocation:** $[AMOUNT or N/A]
**Remote work:** [YES/NO/HYBRID - X days]

Please calculate:
1. **Year 1 total compensation** (including signing bonus, first-year equity vesting)
2. **Steady-state annual compensation** (years 2-4, without one-time bonuses)
3. **Monthly take-home estimate** after taxes in [STATE/PROVINCE]
4. **Hidden value**: Assign a dollar estimate to non-monetary benefits (PTO days, remote work savings, health insurance premium coverage)
5. **Comparison to base**: Show me the "true hourly rate" based on expected hours per week

Note: Calculate Year 1 and steady-state separately because signing bonuses and equity cliff vesting make Year 1 misleading.

</div>

## Prompt #6: Counter-Offer Script Generator

**Best for:** When you've received an offer and need the exact words to negotiate.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT
**Time:** 1-2 minutes

<div class="prompt-block">

I received a job offer and need to negotiate. Generate word-for-word scripts for the following scenarios.

**The offer:**
- Role: [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY]
- Base salary offered: $[AMOUNT]
- My target: $[AMOUNT]
- My anchor (opening ask): $[AMOUNT]

**My leverage:**
- [LIST 2-3 specific leverage factors, e.g., "competing offer at $X from Company Y", "10 years of experience vs. 5 required", "bringing a client list worth $X"]

Generate these four scripts:

**Script 1: Initial Response (buying time)**
What to say when they first present the offer. Goal: express enthusiasm without accepting, buy 48-72 hours to prepare.

**Script 2: The Counter-Offer Conversation**
The phone/video call where I present my counter. Goal: anchor high, justify with evidence, leave room to negotiate.

**Script 3: Handling "This Is Our Best Offer"**
When they say they can't go higher on base. Goal: pivot to other negotiation levers (signing bonus, equity, PTO, title, start date, review timeline).

**Script 4: Accepting the Offer**
What to say and write when we reach agreement. Goal: confirm terms in writing, express genuine excitement, set a positive tone for Day 1.

Keep each script under 150 words. Conversational tone, not corporate. These need to sound like me talking, not a lawyer.

</div>

## Prompt #7: Offer Comparison Matrix

**Best for:** Comparing multiple offers or comparing an offer against your current role.
**Platform:** Claude or ChatGPT
**Time:** 1-2 minutes

<div class="prompt-block">

I'm comparing job offers (or comparing an offer against my current role). Help me make a decision.

**Option A: [COMPANY NAME] (Current Role / Offer 1)**
- Base: $[AMOUNT]
- Bonus: [X]%
- Equity: $[AMOUNT]
- PTO: [X] days
- Remote: [YES/NO/HYBRID]
- Commute: [X] minutes
- Growth potential: [DESCRIBE]
- Manager/team: [DESCRIBE]
- Company stage: [startup/growth/enterprise]
- Job security: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]

**Option B: [COMPANY NAME] (Offer 2)**
[Same fields]

**Option C (if applicable): [COMPANY NAME]**
[Same fields]

**My priorities (ranked):**
1. [e.g., Total compensation]
2. [e.g., Remote flexibility]
3. [e.g., Career growth]
4. [e.g., Work-life balance]
5. [e.g., Job security]

Please create:
1. A side-by-side comparison table with all factors
2. A weighted score based on my priority ranking (score each factor 1-10, apply weights)
3. A "5-year projection" for total compensation at each option (assuming standard raises/promotions)
4. A "regret minimization" analysis: which choice am I least likely to regret in 2 years?
5. Your recommendation with reasoning

</div>

## The Negotiation Principles Behind These Prompts

These prompts give you the data. But data alone doesn't win negotiations. Here are the five principles that make the data work:

### 1. The Anchor Effect

The first number spoken in a negotiation disproportionately influences the outcome. That's why your "anchor" should be 10-20% above your target. If your target is $130K, open at $145K. The final number will gravitate toward whoever sets the anchor.

### 2. Never Accept the First Offer

The first offer is never the best offer. It is the starting point for a conversation. Accepting immediately signals that you would have accepted less, and it can actually make the employer less confident in their choice (they wonder if they overpaid for someone who was desperate).

### 3. The Silence Technique

After you state your counter-offer number, stop talking. Do not fill the silence. Do not justify further. Do not backpedal. Silence creates pressure on the other party to respond. Most people are so uncomfortable with silence that they will improve the offer to fill it.

### 4. Negotiate Beyond Base

If they say "We can't go higher on base," that is not the end. You can negotiate:

- Signing bonus
- Annual bonus target percentage
- Equity/RSU grant
- Extra PTO days
- Remote work flexibility
- Title upgrade
- Earlier performance review (for a raise at 6 months instead of 12)
- Professional development budget
- Relocation assistance
- Start date flexibility

### 5. Always Get It in Writing

Verbal agreements mean nothing. After every negotiation conversation, send a follow-up email summarizing what was discussed and agreed to. Use Prompt #6, Script 4 for this.

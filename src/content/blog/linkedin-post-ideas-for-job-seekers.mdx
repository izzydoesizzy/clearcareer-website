"I don't know what to post."

That's the #1 reason job seekers stay silent on LinkedIn. Not fear of judgment. Not lack of time. They just stare at the blank text box and think: "I have nothing interesting to say."

You do. You just haven't asked yourself the right questions yet.

This resource is a cheat sheet. 30+ story prompts organized by category, plus a simple posting template you can fill in and hit publish. No overthinking. No "building a personal brand." Just showing up, sharing what you know, and letting the right people notice.

## The Simple Posting Template

Every LinkedIn post that performs well follows this structure:

**Line 1:** Hook. Stop the scroll. Make them click "see more."<br />
**Lines 2-8:** Story or insight. One idea. Keep it short.<br />
**Last line:** Takeaway, question, or call to action.

That's it. Here's the fill-in-the-blank version:

> **[One surprising statement or question about your industry/experience]**
>
> [2-3 sentences of context: what happened, what you learned, what you observed]
>
> [The insight or lesson, stated directly]
>
> [A question for your audience OR a simple takeaway they can use]

Keep it under 15 lines. One idea per post. Short paragraphs. Lots of white space.

**Formatting rules:**
- Never put links in the body. Say "link in the comments" and drop it there.
- First line is everything. LinkedIn truncates after ~3 lines. If the hook doesn't make them click "see more," nothing else matters.
- Use line breaks generously. Walls of text get scrolled past.

## How to Use These Prompts

Pick one. Set a timer for 15 minutes. Write a rough draft. Don't edit it to death. Post it.

Your first few posts won't go viral. That's fine. They're not supposed to. They're supposed to make you visible to the 50-100 people who matter: hiring managers, recruiters, and people at your target companies.

Consistency beats perfection. One post a week, every week, is better than one "perfect" post every two months.

## Category 1: Lessons From Your Work

These are the easiest posts to write because you've already lived them.

**Prompt 1:** What's one thing you learned in your last role that surprised you?<br />
*Example hook: "In 3 years managing enterprise accounts, I learned that the #1 predictor of renewal wasn't product satisfaction."*

**Prompt 2:** What's a mistake you made early in your career that taught you something important?<br />
*Example hook: "I once sent a report to the wrong client. What happened next changed how I approach quality checks forever."*

**Prompt 3:** What's one skill you picked up that had nothing to do with your job description but turned out to be incredibly useful?<br />
*Example hook: "Nobody trained me in SQL. I taught myself because I was tired of waiting 3 days for the data team to pull reports."*

**Prompt 4:** What's a process you improved or created that saved your team time?<br />
*Example hook: "I built a spreadsheet that saved my team 4 hours a week. It took me 45 minutes."*

**Prompt 5:** What advice would you give someone starting in your field today?<br />
*Example hook: "If I were starting in [field] today, I'd ignore 90% of the advice online. Here's what I'd actually do."*

**Prompt 6:** What's a common misconception about your role or industry?<br />
*Example hook: "People think [role] is all about [common assumption]. The reality? It's mostly [unexpected truth]."*

**Prompt 7:** What's one tool or system that made you significantly better at your job?<br />
*Example hook: "This one tool changed how I manage my entire workflow. And it's free."*

## Category 2: Industry Observations

Show that you're paying attention to what's happening in your field.

**Prompt 8:** What trend are you noticing in job postings for your role?<br />
*Example hook: "8 out of 10 [role] postings now require [skill]. Two years ago, it was 3 out of 10."*

**Prompt 9:** What's changing in your industry right now that most people aren't talking about?<br />
*Example hook: "Everyone's talking about AI in [industry]. Nobody's talking about [actual shift that matters more]."*

**Prompt 10:** What skill is becoming more valuable in your field, and what skill is becoming less important?<br />
*Example hook: "In [field], knowing [old skill] used to be enough. Now you need [new skill] just to get an interview."*

**Prompt 11:** What did you learn at a recent conference, webinar, or event?<br />
*Example hook: "I attended [event] last week. One speaker said something that completely changed how I think about [topic]."*

**Prompt 12:** What's a prediction you have for your industry in the next 2-3 years?<br />
*Example hook: "Hot take: [role] won't exist in its current form by 2028. Here's what I think replaces it."*

## Category 3: Job Search Transparency

Being open about your search builds empathy, connection, and opportunities.

**Prompt 13:** Share a weekly update on your job search. What happened? What did you learn?<br />
*Example hook: "Week 4 of my job search. Here's what I've learned so far."*

**Prompt 14:** What's one thing about job searching that nobody prepares you for?<br />
*Example hook: "Nobody tells you that job searching is lonely. Like, really lonely."*

**Prompt 15:** Share a rejection and what you took from it.<br />
*Example hook: "I got rejected from a role I really wanted yesterday. Here's what I'm doing with that information."*

**Prompt 16:** What's working in your job search right now? What isn't?<br />
*Example hook: "I've sent 40 applications. 0 responses. Then I tried something different."*

**Prompt 17:** Share something kind that someone did for you during your search.<br />
*Example hook: "A stranger on LinkedIn spent 30 minutes reviewing my resume for free. Here's what they told me."*

**Prompt 18:** What's one thing you wish employers knew about the candidate experience?<br />
*Example hook: "Dear hiring managers: here's what it feels like on the other side of your 6-round interview process."*

## Category 4: Your Expertise and Point of View

Position yourself as someone who thinks deeply about your craft.

**Prompt 19:** What's a problem in your industry that you'd love to solve?<br />
*Example hook: "The biggest problem in [industry] right now isn't [obvious thing]. It's [thing nobody talks about]."*

**Prompt 20:** What's one framework or mental model you use in your work?<br />
*Example hook: "I use a 3-step framework for every [task]. It's saved me from [bad outcome] more times than I can count."*

**Prompt 21:** Share a case study or project you're proud of (without naming confidential details).<br />
*Example hook: "Last year, I led a project that [result]. Here's the approach that made it work."*

**Prompt 22:** What's a book, podcast, or resource that changed how you think about your work?<br />
*Example hook: "This book changed how I approach [skill]. One concept in particular stuck with me."*

**Prompt 23:** What would you do differently if you could redo a past project?<br />
*Example hook: "If I could go back and redo [project], I'd change one thing. And it's not what you'd expect."*

## Category 5: Personal Stories With Professional Lessons

These get the highest engagement because people connect with people, not advice.

**Prompt 24:** What's a non-work experience that taught you something you now use professionally?<br />
*Example hook: "Training for a marathon taught me more about project management than any certification."*

**Prompt 25:** What did your parents or family teach you about work that you still carry with you?<br />
*Example hook: "My dad worked [job] for 30 years. He told me one thing about work that I think about every single week."*

**Prompt 26:** What's a moment in your career where you almost gave up but didn't?<br />
*Example hook: "I almost quit [field] entirely in 2022. Here's what made me stay."*

**Prompt 27:** What's the best career advice you ever received? What's the worst?<br />
*Example hook: "The worst career advice I ever got: 'Just be patient, your turn will come.' The best? 'Nobody's coming to save you.'"*

**Prompt 28:** Share a moment of professional vulnerability that others might relate to.<br />
*Example hook: "I bombed an interview so badly that the interviewer stopped the conversation after 15 minutes. What I did next matters more."*

## Category 6: Open-to-Work Announcements

The highest-stakes post. Do it once and do it right.

**Prompt 29:** Your main open-to-work announcement. Be specific about what you're looking for.<br />
*Template: "After [X years/months] at [company/in field], I'm looking for my next role. I'm targeting [specific role types] at [types of companies]. What I bring: [2-3 specific skills/achievements]. If you know anyone hiring for this kind of role, I'd love an introduction. And if you're in a similar position, drop a comment and let's support each other."*

**Prompt 30:** The follow-up post (2-3 weeks later). Update your network on your search.<br />
*Template: "Quick update on my job search. It's been [X weeks] since I posted that I'm looking. Here's what's happened: [1-2 specific things, could be positive or real]. Here's what I've learned: [insight]. Still looking for [specific role]. If anything comes to mind, I'd appreciate a DM."*

**Prompt 31:** The "I landed" post. Close the loop and thank people.<br />
*Template: "I'm excited to share that I'm joining [company] as [role]. This job search took [timeframe] and I want to share what actually worked: [2-3 things]. Thank you to everyone who [specific help]. If you're still searching, keep going. It works."*

## Bonus: 5 Quick-Win Post Formats

When you're short on time, use these:

**The list post:** "5 things I learned about [topic] this year."<br />
**The hot take:** "Unpopular opinion: [your honest perspective on something in your field]."<br />
**The question:** "Genuine question for people in [field]: How are you handling [specific challenge]?"<br />
**The recommendation:** "If you're in [field] and you're not using [tool/resource], you're missing out. Here's why."<br />
**The gratitude post:** "Shoutout to [person/community] for [specific thing they did]. This is what good networking looks like."

## Your Next Step

Pick one prompt from any category. Set a 15-minute timer. Write it. Post it.

Don't wait until it's perfect. Don't wait until you have 500 connections. Don't wait until you "feel ready."

The best time to start posting was 6 months ago. The second best time is today.

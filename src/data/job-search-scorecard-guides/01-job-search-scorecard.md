# Job Search Scorecard

**10 questions. 5 minutes. Find out exactly where your job search is breaking down.**

Most job seekers know something is not working. They just cannot pinpoint what. Is it the resume? The networking? The interview skills? The mindset? When everything feels broken, you fix nothing because you do not know where to start.

This scorecard diagnoses the problem. Answer 10 questions honestly, score yourself across 5 dimensions, and get a targeted action plan based on your weakest area.

**Time:** 5 minutes to score. 10 minutes to review results. **What you'll need:** Honesty.

## Recommendations by Dimension

### If Strategy scored lowest (below 6)

Your problem is not execution. It is direction. You are applying broadly instead of targeting specifically, and that dilutes everything: your resume, your networking, your interview prep.

**What to do:**
1. Define your target role. Pick 2-3 specific job titles. Not "something in marketing." More like "Product Marketing Manager at B2B SaaS companies with 50-500 employees."
2. Build a target company list of 30-50 companies. Research each one. Know why you want to work there.
3. Set salary expectations using real market data. Know your anchor, target, and walk-away numbers.

**ClearCareer resource:** The Career Change Command Center walks you through strategic targeting from scratch. The Salary Number Generator helps you set your compensation targets.

---

### If Materials scored lowest (below 6)

You might be doing the right things, but your resume and LinkedIn are not converting. Hiring managers see you, but what they see does not compel them to call.

**What to do:**
1. Rewrite your resume with CAR-format achievements (Challenge, Action, Result). Every bullet should show impact, not duties.
2. Optimize your LinkedIn headline, About section, and Experience section for your target role. Use keywords from actual job postings.
3. Get feedback from someone in your target industry. Not your mom. Someone who hires for roles like yours.

**ClearCareer resource:** The Job Search Ignition System includes a professionally written resume, LinkedIn optimization, and 21 total deliverables built specifically for your job search.

---

### If Outreach scored lowest (below 6)

You are relying on job boards and online applications. That is the lowest-conversion channel in job searching. 70-80% of jobs are filled through networking and referrals.

**What to do:**
1. Start sending 5-10 personalized LinkedIn connection requests per week to people at your target companies.
2. Ask for 2 informational interviews per week. Not "are you hiring?" conversations. Genuine curiosity conversations.
3. Build a follow-up system. Track who you have contacted, when, and what the next step is.

**ClearCareer resource:** The Cold Outreach Scripts pack gives you 30 copy-paste templates for cold emails, LinkedIn requests, follow-ups, and referral asks.

---

### If Interview scored lowest (below 6)

You are getting interviews but not converting them to offers. The good news: interview skills are highly trainable. The bad news: you cannot train them in your head. You have to practice out loud.

**What to do:**
1. Build a STAR story bank of 10-12 stories. Write them out. Practice them out loud.
2. Do at least one mock interview per week. With a friend, a coach, or even recording yourself on your phone.
3. Prepare a 60-90 second "Tell me about yourself" answer tailored to your target role.

**ClearCareer resource:** The Interview Confidence Kit includes a story bank builder, the 20 most common questions with frameworks, anxiety management protocols, and post-interview follow-up templates.

---

### If Mindset scored lowest (below 6)

Everything else will underperform if your mindset is suffering. Job searching is emotionally brutal, and most people try to white-knuckle through it without any support system.

**What to do:**
1. Build structure into your search. Set specific hours for job search activities. Do not search all day, every day.
2. Get support. A friend who is also searching, a coach, a therapist, a support group. Job searching in isolation is significantly harder.
3. Separate your identity from your employment status. You are not your job title. Rejection of your application is not rejection of you as a person.

**ClearCareer resource:** The LinkedIn Detox Guide helps you manage social media's impact on your mental health. The Rejection Reframe Cards use CBT techniques to reframe common rejection scenarios.

## Key Takeaway

A job search is a system with five moving parts. When any one part breaks down, the whole system underperforms. You do not need to be perfect at all five. You need to know which one is your bottleneck and fix that first.

Five minutes of honest self-assessment saves weeks of unfocused effort.

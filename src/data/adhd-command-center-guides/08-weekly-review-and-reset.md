# Weekly Review and Reset

**15 minutes every Friday. That is all it takes to stay on track.**

The weekly review is the most important habit in this entire system. Not because it is complicated. Because it is the habit that keeps all the other habits alive. Without a regular check-in, your ADHD brain will drift. Applications will slow down. Networking will stop. The tracker will go untouched. You will not notice until 2-3 weeks have passed and the guilt avalanche buries you.

Fifteen minutes. Every Friday. That is the commitment.

**Time:** 15 minutes, once per week. **What you'll need:** Your application tracker, your CRM, and a timer.

---

## The 15-Minute Friday Review

Set a timer for 15 minutes. Work through these 5 sections in order.

### Section 1: Energy Check-In (2 minutes)

**How was my energy this week? (1-5):** ___

**What drained me most?** ________________

**What energized me most?** ________________

**Adjustment for next week:** ________________
(Example: "I need more breaks between sprints" or "Mornings worked better than afternoons" or "I need to avoid LinkedIn on low-energy days")

### Section 2: Numbers Review (3 minutes)

Fill in your actual numbers:

| Activity | Target | Actual |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Applications submitted | ___ | ___ |
| Networking messages sent | ___ | ___ |
| Conversations/meetings had | ___ | ___ |
| 15-minute sprints completed | ___ | ___ |
| Interview prep sessions | ___ | ___ |

**How do the numbers look?**
- If actual meets or exceeds target: great. Maintain the pace.
- If actual is below target: why? Be specific. Was it energy, overwhelm, avoidance, or life events?
- If actual is way above target: are you approaching burnout? Sustainable pace beats heroic effort.

### Section 3: Pipeline Status (3 minutes)

Update your tracker. For every active application or conversation:

| Company/Role | Status | Next Action | By When |
|-------------|--------|-------------|---------|
| ________________ | ________________ | ________________ | ________________ |
| ________________ | ________________ | ________________ | ________________ |
| ________________ | ________________ | ________________ | ________________ |
| ________________ | ________________ | ________________ | ________________ |

Move items that are done (hired, rejected, ghosted past 3 weeks) to your archive. Keep the active list short and current.

### Section 4: Three Priorities for Next Week (3 minutes)

Pick exactly three priorities for the coming week. Not five. Not "everything." Three.

**Priority 1:** ________________
**Priority 2:** ________________
**Priority 3:** ________________

At least one should be from your weakest dimension this week. If applications were low, make one priority about applications. If networking was zero, make one priority about networking.

### Section 5: Reward Check (2 minutes)

**Points earned this week:** ___

**Reward earned:** ________________

**Did I actually claim my reward?** Yes / No

If no, claim it right now. Rewards only work if you take them. Skipping rewards trains your brain that effort does not pay off, which makes it harder to start next week.

### Section 6: The Reset (2 minutes)

**What am I letting go of from this week?**
Any guilt, any disappointment, any rejection that is still weighing on you. Name it. Then let it go. The new week starts fresh.

________________

**What am I looking forward to next week?**
Name one thing. It does not have to be job-search related.

________________

**Done.** Close the review. Start your weekend.

## Monthly Big Picture Review (30 minutes, once per month)

On the last Friday of each month, add 15 minutes to your weekly review for the bigger-picture check.

### Month-in-Review

**Total applications submitted this month:** ___
**Total conversations/meetings this month:** ___
**Interviews secured this month:** ___
**Offers received this month:** ___

### Trend Check

| Metric | Month 1 | Month 2 | Month 3 | Trend |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| Applications | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up / Flat / Down |
| Conversations | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up / Flat / Down |
| Interviews | ___ | ___ | ___ | Up / Flat / Down |

If interviews are trending down while applications are steady, the problem is your materials or targeting. If applications are trending down, the problem is energy or motivation. If everything is flat, you may need to change your approach.

### Strategy Questions

**Am I targeting the right roles?** If 30+ applications have produced zero interviews, your targeting or resume may need adjustment.

**Am I using the right channels?** If all your applications are cold (job board submissions) and none come from referrals, shift effort toward networking.

**Is my energy sustainable?** If you are burning out, reduce your targets. A lower-volume, longer-duration search beats a high-volume crash.

**Do I need help?** A career coach, a resume reviewer, a therapist, an ADHD coach? There is no shame in getting support. The job search was not designed to be done alone.

### Adjust the Plan

Based on your monthly review, adjust one thing for next month:
- Increase or decrease application targets
- Shift effort between channels (applications vs. networking vs. skill building)
- Add or remove a strategy
- Adjust your reward thresholds

**One adjustment per month.** Not five. One. Small, iterative changes are more sustainable than dramatic overhauls.

## When You Miss a Review

You will miss a Friday. Maybe two Fridays in a row. When you come back:

1. **Do not do a makeup review for missed weeks.** That creates a guilt pile. Just do this week's review.
2. **Do not beat yourself up.** Missing a review is a symptom, not a crime. Note why you missed it (busy, overwhelmed, avoidance?) and address the root cause.
3. **Set a reminder.** If you keep forgetting, set a Friday morning phone alarm labeled "15-min job search review."
4. **Lower the bar.** If 15 minutes feels like too much, do a 5-minute version: energy check, three priorities for next week, done.

The review does not need to be perfect. It needs to happen.

---

## The Review Habit Stack

The easiest way to make the review happen is to attach it to something you already do every Friday.

**Option 1:** "After I finish my last coffee on Friday morning, I do my 15-minute review."

**Option 2:** "Before I start my weekend activities on Friday afternoon, I do my 15-minute review."

**Option 3:** "When my 'Friday Review' alarm goes off at [TIME], I open my tracker and do the review."

Pick one. Use it. The habit stack is more reliable than motivation.

---

## Key Takeaway

The weekly review is a 15-minute investment that prevents weeks of drift. It keeps your search focused, your pipeline current, your energy monitored, and your momentum alive. It also gives you a weekly moment to claim your rewards, let go of the week's weight, and start fresh.

Every Friday. Fifteen minutes. Timer on. Sections 1-6. Reward claimed. Weekend started.

That is the whole system.

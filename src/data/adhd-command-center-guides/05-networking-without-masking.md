# Networking Without Masking

**Build real connections without pretending to be someone you are not.**

Masking is exhausting. ADHD adults spend enormous energy performing neurotypical behavior in social situations: suppressing impulses, monitoring body language, managing eye contact, remembering to ask follow-up questions, not interrupting. After a networking event, you are not just socially drained. You are depleted on a neurological level.

This guide gives you networking strategies that work with your ADHD brain instead of against it. You will learn to manage your social battery, use asynchronous networking (which does not require live social energy), and show up authentically so the connections you build are real.

**Time:** 20 minutes to read. Then apply one strategy this week. **What you'll need:** An understanding of your own social battery and the courage to stop performing.

---

## The Social Battery

### Understand Your Capacity

ADHD social energy works differently from neurotypical social energy. Many ADHD adults are paradoxically social: they love people, get energized by conversations, and then crash hard afterward. Others find social situations draining from the start.

**Rate your social battery capacity:**

| Situation | My Energy Cost (1-5, where 5 is draining) |
|-----------|------------------------------------------|
| 1:1 coffee chat with someone I know | ___ |
| 1:1 coffee chat with a stranger | ___ |
| Small group (3-5 people) networking | ___ |
| Large event (20+ people) | ___ |
| Video call networking | ___ |
| Phone call | ___ |
| Async messaging (LinkedIn, email) | ___ |
| Commenting on social media posts | ___ |

**Your lowest-cost activities are your networking foundation.** Do more of those. Do the high-cost activities only when necessary and budget recovery time afterward.

### The Recovery Rule

After every high-cost social interaction, schedule recovery time. Not "I hope I have time to recover" but actual scheduled time.

| Social Activity Cost | Minimum Recovery |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| 1-2 (low cost) | No recovery needed |
| 3 (moderate) | 30 minutes of quiet |
| 4 (high) | 2-4 hours of low-stimulation time |
| 5 (very high) | Rest of the day. No more social demands. |

If you have a networking event that costs 5, do not schedule a client call or group dinner afterward. Protect the recovery.

## Authentic Networking (No Masking Required)

### Stop Performing "Networker"

The standard networking advice says: make eye contact, firm handshake, ask about their weekend, follow up with a "great to connect" message. That is a performance script. If it does not feel natural to you, it will not come across as natural, and people can tell.

Instead, lean into what ADHD gives you:

**Genuine curiosity.** ADHD brains are wired for curiosity. When something interests you, you ask questions naturally. Find the interesting thing about each person and follow that thread.

**Directness.** Many ADHD adults struggle with small talk but excel at direct, meaningful conversation. Skip the weather. Ask: "What is the most interesting thing you are working on right now?"

**Energy and enthusiasm.** When you are interested, your energy is contagious. Let it show. People remember the person who was genuinely excited about the conversation, not the person who performed polite interest.

**Humor.** ADHD brains make unexpected connections, which often results in humor. Use it. Networking does not have to be serious. People like people who make them laugh.

### What to Let Go Of

- Maintaining eye contact for the "correct" amount of time. Glance away naturally. Nobody is timing you.
- Remembering every person's name. Admit when you forget. "I'm sorry, I'm terrible with names. Can you remind me?" is honest and endearing.
- Following up perfectly. A late follow-up is better than no follow-up. Send it when you remember.
- Staying for the whole event. Leave when your battery is low. Better to have 2 great conversations and leave early than 5 mediocre ones while running on empty.

## Async Networking Strategies

Asynchronous networking (not in real-time) is a game-changer for ADHD adults. It removes time pressure, reduces social energy cost, and lets you respond when your brain is ready.

### Strategy 1: LinkedIn Comment Networking

Instead of attending events, network by commenting thoughtfully on posts from people in your target industry.

- Follow 10-15 people you want to connect with
- Comment on their posts 2-3 times per week
- Add genuine value (your perspective, a question, your experience)
- After 3-4 comments over a few weeks, send a connection request referencing the conversation

This builds familiarity before you ever have a live conversation. When you do reach out, you are not a stranger.

**Time cost:** 10 minutes per day. **Social battery cost:** 1 out of 5.

### Strategy 2: Email/Message First Networking

Instead of meeting people in person first, start the relationship over email or LinkedIn messages.

- Send a personalized message introducing yourself
- Ask a specific question (not "can I pick your brain?")
- Have 2-3 message exchanges before suggesting a call
- By the time you get on the phone, you already have context and rapport

**Time cost:** 15 minutes per message. **Social battery cost:** 1-2 out of 5.

### Strategy 3: Content-Based Networking

Create content (LinkedIn posts, articles, comments) about your target industry. Let people come to you.

- Share insights from your career transition research
- Comment on industry news with your perspective
- Write about what you are learning
- People who engage with your content are pre-qualified networking contacts

**Time cost:** 20-30 minutes per post. **Social battery cost:** 1 out of 5.

## In-Person Networking (When You Must)

Sometimes you need to attend an event. Here is how to survive and even enjoy it.

### Before the Event
- Set a goal: "I will have 2-3 meaningful conversations." Not 10. Not "work the room." Two or three.
- Identify 1-2 people who will be there that you specifically want to meet (check the guest list or LinkedIn).
- Eat before you go (low blood sugar + social demands = meltdown).
- Give yourself permission to leave early.

### During the Event
- Arrive early when the room is less overwhelming and conversations are easier to start.
- Find one person who is standing alone and start there. They are probably as uncomfortable as you are.
- Use the ADHD curiosity advantage: ask genuine questions about what they do. "What is the most interesting thing you are working on?" is better than "What do you do?"
- When a conversation ends naturally, move on. Do not force duration.
- Take breaks. Step outside. Go to the bathroom. Check your phone. Recharge.

### After the Event
- Schedule recovery time (see the Recovery Rule above)
- Send follow-up messages within 48 hours (set a phone reminder)
- Add contacts to your CRM with notes about what you discussed
- Rate your energy: was this event worth the cost? Attend similar ones only if yes.

## The ADHD Networking Advantage

Here is what most networking guides do not tell you: ADHD adults have real advantages in relationship building.

**You remember the interesting stuff.** You might forget someone's name, but you will remember the fascinating detail they shared about their work. People love when you recall the specific, interesting thing they said. "You mentioned that supply chain problem last time, how did that turn out?" is a powerful follow-up.

**You are not boring.** ADHD conversations go places. Your tangents, associations, and unexpected questions make conversations memorable. The person who asked a completely unexpected question is the one who gets remembered.

**You are genuinely enthusiastic.** When you are interested, it shows. And interest is the most flattering thing you can give someone. People want to help people who are genuinely excited about the work.

**You follow up irregularly (and that is fine).** Neurotypical networkers follow up on a strict 30-60-90 day cadence. You follow up when something reminds you of them. "I saw this article and thought of you" sent at a random time is more authentic than a scheduled check-in.

## Weekly Networking Budget

| Activity | Frequency | Social Battery Cost | Time |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|------|
| LinkedIn commenting | Daily | Low | 10 min/day |
| Async messages | 3-5/week | Low | 10 min each |
| 1:1 conversations | 1-2/week | Medium | 15-30 min each |
| In-person events | 1-2/month | High | 2-3 hours each |

Total weekly time: 2-3 hours. Total social battery: manageable if you follow the recovery rule.

---

## Key Takeaway

Networking does not require masking. The ADHD traits that make traditional networking exhausting (social energy limits, difficulty with small talk, impulsiveness) are also the traits that make authentic networking powerful (genuine curiosity, directness, energy, memorable conversations).

Stop trying to network like a neurotypical person. Network like yourself. Lean on async methods. Set boundaries. Recover without guilt. The connections you build authentically will be stronger than any you force.

# Financial Runway for Career Changers

**How much money do you actually need before you can afford to change careers?**

Career change advice usually sounds like this: "Follow your passion! Take the leap! The net will appear!" That is terrible advice if you have rent to pay. And it is especially terrible advice if you have ADHD, because impulsive financial decisions are one of the most common and most destructive symptoms.

This guide is the anti-leap. It helps you calculate exactly how much financial runway you need, build it in an ADHD-friendly way, and create a bridge between your current income and your next career.

**Time:** 60 minutes to work through the numbers. **What you'll need:** Your bank statements, pay stubs, and a calculator.

### The ADHD Tax Line

Notice that "impulse purchases" line. ADHD adults spend an estimated $1,000-$3,000 more per year on impulse purchases than neurotypical adults. Late fees, forgotten subscriptions, duplicate purchases, "retail therapy" dopamine hits. This is real money.

You do not need to eliminate impulse spending (that is unrealistic). But you do need to account for it in your runway calculation. If you budget as though you will spend perfectly, you will run out of money early.

## Step 2: Calculate Your Minimum Viable Income

This is different from your burn rate. Your minimum viable income is the least you can earn per month and still keep the lights on.

Take your burn rate and subtract anything you could temporarily cut:

| Your burn rate | $_____ |
|---|---|
| Minus: dining out / takeout (cook more) | - $_____ |
| Minus: entertainment | - $_____ |
| Minus: shopping (pause non-essentials) | - $_____ |
| Minus: subscriptions you could pause | - $_____ |
| Minus: other cuts you could sustain for 6 months | - $_____ |
| **Minimum Viable Monthly Income** | **$_____** |

Be realistic about what you can cut. ADHD brains need some dopamine sources. If you cut everything enjoyable, you will compensate with bigger impulse spending. Budget for small pleasures.

## Step 3: Build Your Runway

Your runway is how many months you can survive without your current income. You need enough runway to cover the transition period.

### How long does a career change take?

| Transition Type | Typical Timeline |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Same industry, different role | 2-4 months |
| Adjacent industry, similar role | 3-6 months |
| New industry, needs reskilling | 6-12 months |
| Requires certification or degree | 12-24 months |

**Add 50% to whatever number you chose.** Career changes almost always take longer than expected, and ADHD brains are notoriously bad at estimating time.

### The Runway Formula

**Runway needed = (Transition timeline x 1.5) x Minimum viable monthly income**

Example: 6-month transition x 1.5 = 9 months. Minimum viable income of $4,000/month. Runway needed: $36,000.

## Step 4: The Side Hustle Bridge

If you have a gap (most people do), you need income during your transition. Here are side income options ranked by ADHD compatibility.

### High ADHD Compatibility (variety, autonomy, immediate reward)
- **Freelancing your current skills** on platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, or Toptal. You already know how to do it, and projects are short.
- **Tutoring or teaching** in your area of expertise. High social stimulation, clear structure.
- **Consulting on a project basis.** Same skills, different wrapper. Often pays more per hour than your job.
- **Gig work with flexibility.** DoorDash, TaskRabbit, etc. Not glamorous, but immediate cash and you set your own hours.

### Medium ADHD Compatibility (requires some structure)
- **Part-time work in your target industry.** Lower pay but builds experience and network simultaneously.
- **Contract or temp work.** Time-limited, clear scope, often decent pay.
- **Selling things you own.** Facebook Marketplace, eBay, Poshmark. Dopamine from each sale.

### Lower ADHD Compatibility (but might be necessary)
- **Part-time retail or service industry.** Predictable but boring. Use it if you need guaranteed hours.
- **Virtual assistant work.** Can be repetitive but flexible.

**The goal of the bridge is not to replace your income.** It is to extend your runway long enough to complete the transition. Even $1,000-$2,000/month in side income adds 3-6 months to your runway.

## Step 5: ADHD-Friendly Budgeting During Transition

Traditional budgets do not work for ADHD brains. You know this. You have tried. Here are three approaches that actually stick.

### Approach 1: The Two-Account System
Open a second checking account. On payday, transfer your bills amount to Account 1 (bills auto-pay from here). Everything left goes to Account 2 (this is your spending money). When Account 2 is empty, you are done for the month. No tracking required.

### Approach 2: The Weekly Allowance
Divide your monthly spending money by 4. Transfer that amount to your spending account (or withdraw cash) every Monday. This is your budget for the week. Shorter time horizons are easier for ADHD brains to manage.

### Approach 3: The Envelope Method (Digital Version)
Use an app like YNAB or Goodbudget to create virtual envelopes. Groceries, gas, fun money, etc. When an envelope is empty, it is empty. The visual depletion of the "envelope" is more motivating than a number on a spreadsheet.

### Three ADHD Money Rules for Career Transition

**Rule 1: Automate everything that can be automated.** Bills, savings transfers, debt payments. If it requires you to remember to do it, you will eventually forget.

**Rule 2: Add a "whoops" line to your budget.** Budget $200-$400/month for ADHD tax: late fees, impulse buys, forgotten subscriptions. This is not permission to waste money. It is acknowledging reality so it does not wreck your plan.

**Rule 3: Check your accounts every Sunday for 5 minutes.** Set a phone alarm. Open your bank app. Look at the numbers. That is it. Five minutes of awareness per week prevents most financial surprises.

## The Decision Matrix

Before you commit to a career change, make sure the numbers work.

| Condition | Green Light | Yellow Light | Red Light |
|-----------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Runway** | 6+ months covered | 3-5 months covered | Less than 3 months |
| **Debt** | Manageable, not growing | Moderate, can pause | High, growing, urgent |
| **Income bridge** | Have a plan, tested it | Have ideas, not tested | No plan |
| **Partner alignment** | Discussed and agreed | Discussed, concerns | Not discussed |
| **Emergency fund** | 1+ month separate from runway | Partial | None |

**All green:** Go. You are prepared.
**Mix of green and yellow:** Proceed with caution. Build more runway first.
**Any red:** Stop. Fix the red items before making a move.

---

## Key Takeaway

A career change is an investment, not a leap of faith. Like any investment, it requires capital (your runway), a timeline (your transition plan), and risk management (your bridge income).

Do the math first. Then follow your interest. In that order.

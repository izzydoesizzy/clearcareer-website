# The Transferable Skills Translator

**You have more skills than you think. Most of them just do not have names yet.**

Career changers always underestimate their transferable skills. ADHD career changers underestimate them even more, because half of your best skills developed as coping mechanisms. You do not think of them as skills. You think of them as survival.

This guide helps you identify, name, and translate your existing skills into the language of your target career. You will also discover the ADHD advantage skills that most people do not have and most employers do not know they need.

**Time:** 45-60 minutes. **What you'll need:** Your resume, a list of your last 3-5 jobs, and your Dopamine Career Map from Guide 02.

## The ADHD Advantage Skills

These skills are disproportionately common in adults with ADHD and disproportionately valuable in the workplace. If you have them, name them and claim them.

| ADHD Advantage Skill | What It Looks Like at Work | Industries That Value It |
|---------------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| **Hyperfocus** | Deep work for hours on engaging problems | Tech, research, design, writing |
| **Pattern recognition** | Seeing connections others miss | Strategy, analytics, consulting |
| **Crisis performance** | Calm and effective under pressure | Healthcare, events, sales, startups |
| **Rapid ideation** | Generating many ideas quickly | Marketing, product, entrepreneurship |
| **Adaptive thinking** | Pivoting instantly when plans change | Project management, consulting |
| **High empathy** | Reading people and emotional situations | HR, therapy, teaching, sales |
| **Risk tolerance** | Comfortable with ambiguity and uncertainty | Startups, entrepreneurship, investing |
| **Fast learning** | Picking up new domains quickly | Consulting, tech, generalist roles |
| **Energy bursts** | Intense productivity in sprints | Any deadline-driven work |
| **Divergent thinking** | Approaching problems from unusual angles | Innovation, R&D, creative fields |

**Circle the ones you have.** Then add them to your skills inventory.

## The Gap Analysis

After translating your skills, you will notice gaps. Skills the target career requires that you do not have yet. This is normal and expected.

List your gaps:
1. ________________
2. ________________
3. ________________
4. ________________
5. ________________

Now categorize each gap:

**Quick to close (weeks):** Can learn through online courses, YouTube, or self-study.
**Medium effort (months):** Needs structured learning, a certificate, or a project.
**Major investment (years):** Requires formal education or extensive experience.

The "80% ready" rule: if you have 80% of the skills for a role, you are ready to apply. The last 20% you can learn on the job. Do not wait until you have 100%. You will never feel 100% ready. Nobody does.

---

## Key Takeaway

You are not starting from zero. You are starting from 15-20 years of accumulated skills, half of which you have never named and most of which translate directly to your new career.

Name them. Translate them. Own them.

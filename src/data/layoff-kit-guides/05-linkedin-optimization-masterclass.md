# LinkedIn Optimization Masterclass

Position your LinkedIn profile for a job search after a layoff, without looking desperate.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general career advice. Results vary by industry, target roles, and individual circumstances.

You don't need to do everything today. Work through this at your own pace.

**Time estimate:** 2-3 hours for full optimization
**What you'll need:** Your updated resume, a professional headshot, and 3-5 job postings for your target roles

**Top 3 actions from this guide:**
1. Replace your headline immediately. The default "Former [Title] at [Company]" hurts you in every search result.
2. Rewrite your About section using the 4-paragraph template in this guide.
3. Turn on "Open to Work" in recruiter-only mode after your profile is updated.

---

## The Golden Rule: Optimize Before You Signal

Do not post "I've been laid off" or turn on "Open to Work" before your profile is ready. Take a couple of hours to get it right first. You can always refine later.

**Action:** Complete every section below before enabling Open to Work or posting anything about your transition.

---

## Headline (The Most Important Line)

Your headline appears in search results, connection requests, comments, and messages. The default ("Former [Title] at [Company]") is the worst possible headline after a layoff.

### 5 Formulas That Work

```
Formula 1: What You Do + Who You Help
Senior Product Manager | Building products that solve real problems for enterprise SaaS teams

Formula 2: Function + Industry + Specialty
Operations Leader | Manufacturing & Supply Chain | Lean Six Sigma Black Belt

Formula 3: Outcome-Focused
I help B2B companies grow revenue through strategic partnerships and channel sales

Formula 4: Title + Expertise + Signal
VP Finance | FP&A, M&A, Capital Markets | Open to Senior Finance Leadership

Formula 5: Transition-Friendly
Marketing Director | 12 years in CPG brand strategy | Open to Brand Strategy roles in CPG
```

**Avoid:** "Unemployed," "Seeking New Opportunities," "Thought Leader," "Passionate," "Guru," "Ninja." The green badge already signals you're looking. Don't say it twice.

**Action:** Write your headline using one of the five formulas above.

## About Section (Your Elevator Pitch)

Most people leave this blank or write a boring summary. Use this 4-paragraph structure:

```
Paragraph 1: What you do and why it matters (2-3 sentences)
"I've spent 15 years building and scaling operations teams in the
logistics industry. From a 20-person warehouse startup to a 500-person
distribution network, my focus has always been the same: making complex
systems run smoothly so the business can grow without the chaos."

Paragraph 2: Key accomplishments (3-4 bullets)
"What I'm known for:
- Reducing fulfillment costs by 22% across a $180M operation
- Building a forecasting model that improved inventory accuracy from 78% to 96%
- Leading a cross-border expansion into 3 new markets in 18 months"

Paragraph 3: What you're looking for (2 sentences)
"I'm currently exploring senior operations leadership roles in logistics,
supply chain, or distribution. I'm especially interested in companies
going through growth transitions where operational complexity is the bottleneck."

Paragraph 4: How to reach you (1 sentence)
"If you're building something interesting, I'd love to hear about it.
Reach me at [email] or send a connection request."
```

**Action:** Write your About section using this template.

## Open to Work Strategy

LinkedIn has two modes:

**Recruiter Only (use first):** Only people with LinkedIn Recruiter licenses can see it. No green banner.

**Public Green Banner:** Everyone sees it. Signals broadly that you're looking.

**Timing:**
1. Weeks 1-2: Optimize your profile. Don't turn on Open to Work yet.
2. Weeks 2-4: Enable "Recruiter Only" mode. Start reaching out to your network.
3. Week 4+: Consider switching to the public green banner for broader visibility.

This timing is for LinkedIn visibility only. Continue applying through other channels from day one, especially if you're receiving EI.

**Action:** Set Open to Work to "Recruiter Only" after your profile is fully optimized.

## Experience Section

Your most recent role should tell a story of impact, not list responsibilities.

```
Don't write: "Managed a team of 8 analysts"
Do write:    "Built and led an 8-person analytics team that delivered
              $12M in identified cost savings over 3 years"

Don't write: "Responsible for budget management"
Do write:    "Managed a $45M operating budget, consistently delivering
              within 2% of forecast"
```

For your most recent role, add a note about the transition:

```
Note: My role was eliminated as part of a company-wide restructuring
in [month/year]. I'm now actively exploring my next opportunity.
```

Factual, professional, stigma removed.

**Action:** Rewrite your 3 most recent experience entries with impact-focused bullets.

## Skills & Endorsements

LinkedIn's algorithm uses your skills section for search matching. This directly affects whether recruiters find you.

1. Add 15-20 relevant skills (focus on the top ones, not all 50)
2. Pin your top 3 skills (most relevant to your target role)
3. Ask 5-10 colleagues to endorse your key skills
4. Remove outdated or irrelevant skills

**Pro tip:** Look at job postings for your target roles. Note the skills they mention. Add those exact keywords to your profile.

**Action:** Add 15+ skills, pin your top 3, and send 5 endorsement requests this week.

## Content Strategy During Job Search

Posting keeps you visible and demonstrates expertise. Here's what to post and what to avoid.

| Post Type | Example | Frequency |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Industry insight | "3 trends I'm watching in [your industry]" | 1x/week |
| Lesson learned | "One thing I learned managing a $50M P&L" | 1x/week |
| Helpful resource | Share an article with your take | 1x/week |
| Transition update | "I'm exploring [type of role]. Here's what I'm looking for." | 1x/month |

**Never post:** Anything negative about your former employer, "Day 47 of unemployment" counters, vague "exciting things coming" teasers, excessive gratitude posts, or political takes.

### The Network Activation Post

When you're ready to tell your network:

```
What happened (1 sentence, factual):
"After [X years] at [Company], my role was eliminated as part of a restructuring."

What you're looking for (2-3 sentences, specific):
"I'm exploring [type of role] in [industry/function]. I'm especially
interested in [specific detail about what you want]."

How people can help (1-2 sentences, actionable):
"If you know someone at [type of company] or have a connection in
[specific area], I'd love an introduction. Feel free to DM me."
```

This generates significantly more engagement than a generic "I'm looking for a job" post because it gives people a specific way to help.

**Action:** Draft your network activation post now. Save it until your profile is fully optimized.

## Profile Optimization Checklist

Run through this before activating Open to Work:

- [ ] Professional headshot (not a selfie, not a group photo crop)
- [ ] Custom headline (not default job title)
- [ ] About section written (4-paragraph template)
- [ ] Current role updated with impact-focused bullets
- [ ] 15+ relevant skills added and top 3 pinned
- [ ] Custom URL set (linkedin.com/in/yourname)
- [ ] Location set to your target job market
- [ ] Industry set correctly
- [ ] Contact info visible (email, phone optional)
- [ ] Banner image updated (professional, not default blue). Use a simple image related to your industry. Canva has free LinkedIn banner templates.

**Action:** Complete every checkbox before you turn on Open to Work or post your transition announcement.

---

## Key Takeaway

Your LinkedIn profile is a landing page, not an online resume. It should tell a clear story, highlight specific impact, and make it easy for the right people to find and contact you. Spend 2-3 hours optimizing it before you reach out to anyone.

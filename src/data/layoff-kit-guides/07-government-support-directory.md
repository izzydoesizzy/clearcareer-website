# Government Support & Retraining Directory

**Canadian government programs, grants, and resources that may be available to you after a layoff.**

> **Important:** Government program details, rates, eligibility requirements, and funding levels change regularly. All figures in this guide are as of April 2026 unless otherwise noted. Verify current information with the official government sources linked in each section before making decisions.

Many people apply for EI and stop there. But there are dozens of federal and provincial programs designed to help you during a career transition. Some provide cash. Some provide free training. Some provide both. Here's the complete list.

---

## Federal Programs

### Employment Insurance (EI) Regular Benefits

The main safety net. Apply immediately at [canada.ca/ei](https://www.canada.ca/en/services/benefits/ei/ei-regular-benefit.html).

| Detail | 2026 Rules |
|--------|-----------|
| Benefit rate | 55% of average insurable earnings |
| Maximum weekly benefit | $729/week ($37,908/year) |
| Maximum insurable earnings | $68,900 |
| Qualifying hours | 420-700 (varies by regional unemployment rate) |
| Benefit duration | 14-45 weeks |
| Waiting period | Waived through Oct 10, 2026 (temporary measure) |
| Severance interaction | No delay/reduction through Oct 10, 2026 (temporary measure) |

**Pro tips:**
- Apply the day you're terminated. Don't wait for your ROE.
- Report all earnings while on EI (you can earn up to 25% of your weekly benefit or $50 before deductions)
- If you're over 60 and have 600+ hours in 3 of the last 5 years, you may qualify for up to 20 extra weeks

### Canada Training Credit

You've been accumulating $250/year in training credit since 2020 (up to $5,000 lifetime). Check your balance on CRA My Account.

- Covers 50% of eligible tuition and fees
- Can be used for courses at designated educational institutions
- Available for workers aged 26-65 with income between $10,000 and ~$150,000

### Canada Workers Benefit (CWB)

If your income drops significantly, you may qualify for the CWB, a refundable tax credit of up to ~$1,428 for individuals or ~$2,461 for families. Claimed on your tax return.

### Workforce Development Agreements

Federal funding flows to provinces for employment services. Each province delivers these differently. See the provincial sections below.

## Ontario Programs

### Employment Ontario

Free employment services including career counseling, job search workshops, resume help, and employer connections.

- **Find a centre:** [ontario.ca/employmentontario](https://www.ontario.ca/page/employment-ontario)
- Services are free and available to all Ontario residents

### Second Career Program

Provides up to $28,000 for retraining in an in-demand occupation.

- Must be laid off and unable to find employment in your field
- Covers tuition, books, transportation, and basic living expenses
- Training must lead to employment in a high-demand field
- Apply through your local Employment Ontario office
- [ontario.ca/secondcareer](https://www.ontario.ca/page/second-career)

### Ontario Skills Development Fund

Employer-led training programs. Not individual applications, but check if your industry association has a funded program through this stream.

### Micro-credentials

Ontario is funding short-term credential programs (6-12 weeks) through colleges. Many are free or heavily subsidized. Check:
- [ontariocolleges.ca](https://www.ontariocolleges.ca) for short programs
- [eCampusOntario](https://www.ecampusontario.ca) for online micro-credentials

## British Columbia Programs

### WorkBC Employment Services

BC's employment service network. Free career planning, job search, training, and wage subsidies.

- **Find a centre:** [workbc.ca/find-a-centre](https://www.workbc.ca/Employment-Services/Find-a-WorkBC-Centre.aspx)
- Includes skills training funding (up to $10,000 for some programs)

### BC Employer Training Grant

Employers can apply for up to $10,000 per employee for skills training. If you're transitioning to a new employer, ask if they'll apply on your behalf.

### StrongerBC Future Skills Grant

Up to $3,500 for short-term skills training.
- For BC residents aged 19+
- Covers micro-credentials, certificates, and short courses
- [workbc.ca/futureskills](https://www.workbc.ca)

## Alberta Programs

### Training for Work

Alberta's main employment training program for unemployed Albertans.

- Funded training programs ranging from 3 weeks to 12 months
- Includes workplace training, occupational training, and self-employment
- Apply through Alberta Supports centres
- [alberta.ca/training-for-work](https://www.alberta.ca/training-for-work.aspx)

### Canada-Alberta Job Grant

Up to $10,000 per trainee for employer-led training. Useful if you're transitioning to a new employer.

### Alberta Supports

Financial assistance for basic needs during unemployment. Income-tested.
- [alberta.ca/alberta-supports](https://www.alberta.ca/alberta-supports.aspx)

## Quebec Programs

### Emploi-Quebec

Quebec's employment service network. Services in French and English.

- Free career counseling and job search assistance
- Training subsidies for skills upgrading
- [quebec.ca/emploi](https://www.quebec.ca/en/employment)

### Workforce Training Programs (Commission des partenaires du marche du travail)

Industry-specific training programs funded through Quebec's 1% training tax.

## EI While Training

You can attend training while on EI, but the rules differ:

**Self-directed training:** You can take courses on your own time without affecting EI, as long as you remain available for work and continue your job search.

**Full-time training:** If you're enrolled full-time in a training program, you need EI approval (through a referral from an employment counselor) to continue receiving benefits during training. This is called an EI-funded training program.

**Action:** Before enrolling in any full-time program, visit your local Service Canada office and ask about EI eligibility during training.

## Key Takeaway

There's more support available than most people realize. Between EI, provincial training programs, and professional development resources, you can fund a significant career upgrade during your transition. The key is knowing what exists and applying early. Most programs have limited spots and funding cycles.

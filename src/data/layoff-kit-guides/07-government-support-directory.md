# Government Support & Retraining Directory

**Every Canadian program, grant, and resource you might qualify for after a layoff.**

> **Disclaimer:** Program details, rates, and eligibility change regularly. All figures are as of April 2026. Verify with official government sources before making decisions.

You don't need to do everything today. Work through this at your own pace.

**Time:** 20 minutes to scan, then follow up on what applies to you. **What you'll need:** Your province, your most recent pay stub (for EI), and your CRA My Account login.

**Top 3 actions from this guide:**
1. Apply for EI today at canada.ca/ei. Do not wait for your Record of Employment.
2. Log into CRA My Account and check your Canada Training Credit balance.
3. Call 211 to find local employment programs, financial assistance, and retraining in your area.

---

## Apply for EI First

This is the most important thing. Do it today.

Apply at [canada.ca/ei](https://www.canada.ca/en/services/benefits/ei/ei-regular-benefit.html). Don't wait for your Record of Employment (ROE).

| Detail | 2026 Rules |
|--------|-----------|
| Benefit rate | 55% of average insurable earnings |
| Maximum weekly | $729/week ($3,159/month) — verify at canada.ca/ei as rates change annually |
| Max insurable earnings | $68,900 — verify at canada.ca/ei |
| Qualifying hours | 420-700 — your required hours depend on your region's unemployment rate. Verify at canada.ca/ei |
| Benefit duration | 14-45 weeks |
| Waiting period | Waived through Oct 10, 2026 (temporary measure) — verify at canada.ca/ei |
| Severance interaction | No delay or reduction through Oct 10, 2026 (temporary measure) — verify at canada.ca/ei |

**Tips:**
- Apply the day you're terminated.
- Report all earnings while on EI. You can earn up to 25% of your weekly benefit (or $50) before deductions.
- If you're over 60 with 600+ hours in 3 of the last 5 years, you may qualify for up to 20 extra weeks.

**Action:** Apply for EI right now if you haven't already.

## Check Your Canada Training Credit

You've been accumulating $250/year since 2019 (for the 2020 tax year) (up to $5,000 lifetime). Log into [CRA My Account](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/e-services/e-services-individuals/account-individuals.html) to check your balance.

- Covers 50% of eligible tuition and fees
- For workers aged 26-65 with income between $10,000 and ~$150,000
- Use it for courses at designated educational institutions

**Action:** Check your balance today. It takes 2 minutes.

## Other Federal Programs

**Canada Workers Benefit (CWB):** If your income drops significantly, you may qualify for up to ~$1,428 (individuals) or ~$2,461 (families). Claimed on your tax return.

## Ontario Programs

**Employment Ontario** - Free career counseling, job search workshops, resume help. Find a centre: [ontario.ca/employmentontario](https://www.ontario.ca/page/employment-ontario)

**Second Career Program** - Up to $28,000 for retraining in an in-demand occupation. Covers tuition, books, transportation, and basic living expenses. Apply through your local Employment Ontario office. [ontario.ca/secondcareer](https://www.ontario.ca/page/second-career)

**Micro-credentials** - Free or heavily subsidized short programs (6-12 weeks) through Ontario colleges. Check [ontariocolleges.ca](https://www.ontariocolleges.ca) and [eCampusOntario](https://www.ecampusontario.ca).

---

## British Columbia Programs

**WorkBC** - Free career planning, job search, training, and wage subsidies. Up to $10,000 for some training programs. Find a centre: [workbc.ca](https://www.workbc.ca/Employment-Services/Find-a-WorkBC-Centre.aspx)

**StrongerBC Future Skills Grant** - Up to $3,500 for short-term skills training. For BC residents aged 19+. Covers micro-credentials, certificates, and short courses.

---

## Alberta Programs

**Training for Work** - Funded training programs (3 weeks to 12 months) for unemployed Albertans. Apply through Alberta Supports centres. [alberta.ca/training-for-work](https://www.alberta.ca/training-for-work.aspx)

**Canada-Alberta Job Grant** - Up to $10,000 per trainee for employer-led training. Useful if you're starting with a new employer.

**Alberta Supports** - Financial assistance for basic needs during unemployment. Income-tested. [alberta.ca/alberta-supports](https://www.alberta.ca/alberta-supports.aspx)

---

## Quebec Programs

**Emploi-Quebec** - Free career counseling and job search assistance in French and English. Training subsidies for skills upgrading. [quebec.ca/emploi](https://www.quebec.ca/en/employment)

**Programme d'aide a l'emploi (PAEF)** - Financial assistance and employment support for job seekers. Includes wage subsidies for employers who hire, training allowances, and support for self-employment projects. Apply through your local Centre local d'emploi (CLE).

## EI While Training

**Part-time courses:** You can take courses while on EI as long as you remain available for work and continue job searching.

**Full-time training:** You need EI approval (through an employment counselor referral) to keep receiving benefits during full-time training.

Your spouse's income does not affect your EI eligibility. Some provincial programs are income-tested at the household level.

**Action:** Before enrolling in any full-time program, visit your local Service Canada office and ask about EI eligibility during training.

## Key Takeaway

There's more support available than most people realize. Between EI, provincial training programs, and free online resources, you can fund a significant career upgrade during your transition. The key is knowing what exists and applying early. Most programs have limited spots.

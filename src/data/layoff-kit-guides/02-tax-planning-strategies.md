# Tax Planning Strategies for Severance

Reduce the tax hit on your severance by structuring it before the cheque is cut.

> **Disclaimer:** This is general tax education, not tax advice. Consult a CPA before implementing any strategy. Results vary by income, province, and personal circumstances.

You don't need to do everything today. Work through this at your own pace.

**Time estimate:** 30 minutes to read, 1-2 hours with your accountant
**What you'll need:** Your latest Notice of Assessment (or CRA My Account login), your severance offer letter, and a list of any equity compensation

**Top 3 actions from this guide:**
1. Ask your employer to transfer severance directly into your RRSP before the cheque is cut.
2. If laid off in Q4, ask about splitting the payment across two tax years to stay in a lower bracket.
3. Log into CRA My Account and check your RRSP and TFSA contribution room right now.

---

## How Severance Is Taxed in Canada

Lump-sum severance gets tax withheld at source before you see a dollar.

| Lump Sum Amount | Federal Withholding | Quebec Withholding |
|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Up to $5,000 | 10% | 5% |
| $5,001 - $15,000 | 20% | 10% |
| Over $15,000 | **30%** | **15%** (+ provincial) |

**Note:** The Quebec rates shown above are provincial withholding only. Federal withholding applies on top. Provincial tax is also on top in other provinces. A $120K severance in Ontario means roughly $36,000-$40,000 withheld at source.

The withholding rate is not your actual tax rate. Your real marginal rate may be higher (43-53% for high earners) or lower (if severance is your only income that year). The difference gets settled when you file.

**Action:** Log into CRA My Account and check your RRSP and TFSA contribution room right now. You'll need these numbers for the strategies below.

## Strategy 1: RRSP Direct Transfer

The most powerful tax strategy for severance. Direct your employer to transfer part or all of your severance into your RRSP before the cheque is cut.

When the employer transfers directly, no tax is withheld at source. You keep 100% of the transferred amount, and it reduces your taxable income.

**Example:**
- Severance: $120,000
- RRSP room: $45,000
- Direct transfer to RRSP: $45,000 (no tax withheld)
- Remaining lump sum: $75,000 (30% withheld = $22,500)
- Savings vs. full lump sum: ~$13,500 in withholding you keep now, plus long-term tax-deferred growth

**How to do it:**

```
Email to employer (send BEFORE severance is processed):

"I would like $[amount] of my severance transferred directly to my
RRSP at [institution name], account number [XXXX]. Please confirm
this will be processed as a direct transfer with no tax withholding."
```

Do not transfer more than your available RRSP room. Over-contributions are penalized at 1% per month by CRA.

**Action:** Send this request to your employer's payroll department before the severance payment is processed. Once the cheque is cut, you lose the withholding benefit.

## Strategy 2: Retiring Allowance Rollover

If your severance is classified as a "retiring allowance" (most are), and you have pre-1996 service years, you get extra RRSP room above your normal contribution limit.

- $2,000 per year of service before 1996
- $1,500 per year of service before 1989 where employer pension contributions didn't vest

**Example:** Started work in 1992 = 4 pre-1996 years = $8,000 extra RRSP room.

This only applies if you started working before 1996 (30+ years of experience by 2026).

Ask your employer's payroll department to include this in the direct RRSP transfer. If they're unfamiliar, point them to CRA Guide T4040.

**Action:** Calculate your pre-1996 service years and tell payroll to include the retiring allowance rollover in your RRSP transfer.

## Strategy 3: Timing Across Tax Years

If you're laid off in October, November, or December, ask your employer to defer part of the payment to January. Splitting income across two tax years can keep you in a lower bracket both years.

**Example:**
- Laid off November 2026, severance $120,000
- Paid in full in 2026: pushed into ~53% combined marginal bracket (this rate applies to income above approximately $235,000 in Ontario)
- Split $60K in December + $60K in January: each year stays in a lower bracket

```
Script for employer:

"Can we structure the severance as salary continuation through
[date in January] rather than a lump sum? This would split the
payment across two tax years."
```

This only helps if you expect lower income the following year. If you start a high-paying job in January, splitting may not save anything.

**Action:** If your layoff is in Q4, ask about deferring part of the payment to January before signing.

## Strategy 4: Salary Continuation vs. Lump Sum

Some employers offer severance as monthly payments instead of a lump sum. Each has trade-offs.

**Salary continuation pros:**
- Tax withheld at your normal payroll rate (may be lower than lump-sum rates)
- Benefits typically continue during the continuation period
- CPP/EI premiums continue to accumulate

**Salary continuation cons:**
- Employer may stop payments if you find a new job
- You lose control of the money
- If the employer goes bankrupt, you may lose unpaid amounts

**Lump sum pros:**
- Full amount immediately, full control
- No employer bankruptcy risk

You can sometimes negotiate a hybrid: part lump sum (for RRSP transfer), part salary continuation (for benefits and lower withholding).

**Action:** Ask your employer which options are available and compare them with your CPA.

## Strategy 5: TFSA as a Tax-Free Bridge

Not a tax savings strategy, but a planning tool. TFSA withdrawals are completely tax-free.

If you have TFSA savings, draw from them for living expenses and direct the severance into your RRSP. You live on tax-free money while sheltering severance from tax. 2026 TFSA contribution limit: $7,000/year (cumulative lifetime limit depends on your age). Confirm the current year's limit at your CRA My Account.

**Action:** If you have TFSA savings and RRSP room, use TFSA for expenses and shelter severance in your RRSP.

## Strategy 6: Stock Options and RSUs

If you have equity compensation, termination triggers tax events.

**RSUs:** Unvested RSUs are typically forfeited on termination. If you negotiate accelerated vesting, the vested shares are taxable income at market value on the vesting date.

**Stock Options:** Check your exercise deadline post-termination (usually 30-90 days). Exercising in-the-money options creates a taxable benefit. The employee stock option deduction (50% of the benefit) may apply for CCPC shares. Stock option deduction rules changed in 2024. For non-CCPC shares, the 50% deduction may be capped at $250K. Consult a tax professional.

**Action:** Check your equity agreement for post-termination rules, note your exercise deadline, and talk to a tax professional before exercising or selling.

---

## Strategy 7: Employment Insurance Interaction

EI does not reduce your severance, but severance can affect your EI start date.

Under current temporary measures (valid through October 10, 2026), severance payments do not delay or reduce EI benefits. Verify the current status of these temporary measures at canada.ca/ei before relying on them. Policies can change. After October 2026, if temporary measures end, lump-sum severance gets allocated over your notice period and delays EI accordingly.

**Action:** Apply for EI immediately regardless of severance. Service Canada sorts out the timing.

## When to Hire an Accountant

A one-time CPA consultation costs $200-$500 and is worth it if:

- Severance is over $50,000
- You have stock options or RSUs
- You have significant RRSP room
- You're choosing between salary continuation and lump sum
- You have other income sources (rental, business, investment)

Ask for a "severance tax planning consultation." Any experienced Canadian CPA will know what this means.

**Action:** Book a CPA appointment before your severance payment is processed. Many of these strategies must be arranged in advance.

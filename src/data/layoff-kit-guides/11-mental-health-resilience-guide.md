# Mental Health & Resilience

**Quick steps, real resources, and tools to protect your mental health after a layoff.**

> **If you're in crisis, call or text 988.** Full list of crisis lines is below.

Resources in this guide are specific to Canada.

**Time:** 10 minutes to read, then bookmark what you need.

---

## If You Just Found Out

Take 5 slow breaths. In through your nose, out through your mouth.

Drink some water.

You don't have to do anything else right now. When you're ready, keep reading.

---

## What You're Feeling Is Normal

Layoff grief is real. You lost income, routine, identity, and social connection all at once. Here's a quick map:

| Feeling | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Numbness | Your brain is protecting you |
| Anger | You're processing unfairness |
| Shame | You've tied your worth to your job (most professionals do) |
| Relief, then guilt | The job wasn't right for you |
| Anxiety | You're facing real uncertainty |

All of these are normal. None of them mean you failed.

You might also notice physical reactions: racing heart, tight chest, nausea, shaking hands. This is your body's stress response. It's uncomfortable but not dangerous. If symptoms persist, see a doctor.

Many people report weeks 2-4 as the hardest. But everyone is different. If you're struggling longer, it doesn't mean something is wrong. It means you should talk to someone. By week 6-8, the intensity usually starts to lift for most people. If it hasn't by then, reach out to one of the resources below.

---

## When to Get Help

Most post-layoff stress is temporary. But if any of these last more than 2 weeks, talk to a professional:

- Can't sleep, or sleeping 12+ hours a day
- Can't get out of bed or do basic tasks
- Avoiding everyone, not answering calls
- Drinking more than usual
- Feeling hopeless ("this will never get better")
- Significant appetite or weight changes

Getting help is not weakness. It's using resources, same as hiring a lawyer for your severance.

## First Steps

You don't have to do everything today. Pick one. Start there.

- [ ] Tell one person what happened (partner, friend, family member)
- [ ] Move your body for 20+ minutes (walk, run, gym, anything)
- [ ] Check if your former employer's EAP (Employee Assistance Program) is still active (call the number on your old benefits card. Many employers extend access after termination, but the window varies. Call to confirm how long you have.)
- [ ] Set a daily wake-up time and stick to it, even if you have nowhere to be
- [ ] When you do start searching (not today, not this week), limit it to 5 hours/day max. Set a stop time. Respect it.
- [ ] Check your benefits booklet for psychology/counseling coverage and book sessions before your benefits end
- [ ] If your employer offered outplacement services as part of your severance, use them. They're already paid for and often include counseling.

If money stress is keeping you up at night, start with the Financial Runway Worksheet in this kit to get your actual numbers. For benefits and coverage questions, see the Benefits Transition Guide. When you're ready to structure your days, the Job Search Launch Plan has a daily framework.

### Ongoing Habits

These are repeatable. Come back to this list each week.

- [ ] Exercise 3+ times this week
- [ ] At least one social interaction (not networking, real connection)
- [ ] Used my stop time every day
- [ ] Did the 3-good-things practice at least once

## Daily Mental Health Toolkit

**The 5-minute reset:** When anxiety spikes, do box breathing. Inhale 4 seconds, hold 4 seconds, exhale 4 seconds, hold 4 seconds. Repeat 4 times.

**The worry window:** Give yourself 15 minutes per day to worry about money or the job search. Set a timer. When it's done, move on. Outside that window, redirect. This is a real CBT technique.

**The 3-good-things practice:** Before bed, write down 3 things that went well today. They can be small. This rewires your brain's negativity bias over time.

**Move your body:** Exercise isn't just good for your body. Research suggests regular physical activity can be as effective as some treatments for mild-to-moderate depression. It's a powerful complement to professional care, not a replacement. A daily 20-minute walk makes a measurable difference.

**The social minimum:** Schedule at least 2 social interactions per week. Not networking. Real human connection. Isolation is the biggest mental health risk after a layoff.

**The stop time:** Pick a time every day when you're done with job search activities. 3:30 PM. 4:00 PM. Whatever works. After that, you are off the clock. You are not a job search machine.

**Protect your sleep:** Same bedtime, same wake time, even on weekends. No screens 30 minutes before bed. No caffeine after 2 PM.

**If you have a partner:** Schedule one money conversation per week. Not daily check-ins, not reactive arguments. One planned conversation. The Financial Runway Worksheet in this kit gives you the numbers.

## Free Counseling and Therapy (Canada)

| Resource | What You Get | How to Access |
|---|---|---|
| Your old employer's EAP (Employee Assistance Program) | 6-8 free sessions. Many employers extend access after termination, but the window varies. | Call the number on your benefits card to confirm how long you have |
| [BounceBack (CMHA)](https://bouncebackontario.ca) | Free guided CBT, phone coaching + workbooks | Ontario: [bouncebackontario.ca](https://bouncebackontario.ca), BC: [bouncebackbc.ca](https://bouncebackbc.ca) |
| [AbilitiCBT](https://www.abiliticbt.com) | Free online CBT therapy | Available in ON, MB, SK, NL, NS, NB, PE, QC. Province availability changes. Check abiliticbt.com for current coverage. |
| [Walk-in counseling clinics](https://walkincounselling.com) | Single-session counseling, no appointment | Ontario directory, most major cities |
| University training clinics | Therapy with supervised grad students | $20-$60/session, contact local university psych dept |
| [Credit Counselling Canada](https://creditcounsellingcanada.ca) | Free financial counseling (non-profit, all provinces) | creditcounsellingcanada.ca |
| [Open Path Collective](https://openpathcollective.org) | Sliding-scale therapy | $30-$80 USD/session |

## Mental Health Apps

These are tools for general wellbeing, not replacements for professional care. If you're struggling, talk to a real person first.

| Tool | What It Does | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| [MindShift CBT](https://www.anxietycanada.com/resources/mindshift-cbt/) | Anxiety management tools from Anxiety Canada | Free |
| [Insight Timer](https://insighttimer.com) | Guided meditation, sleep, and breathing exercises (70,000+ free sessions) | Free |
| [Woebot](https://woebothealth.com) | AI-based CBT chatbot for anxiety and mood (available in Canada but content is US-focused) | Free |
| [Daylio](https://daylio.net) | Mood tracking without writing (tap-based journal) | Free, premium $5/year |

**Paid options:** [Calm](https://calm.com) and [Headspace](https://headspace.com) both offer guided meditation and mindfulness. Free trial, then ~$70/year.

## Find a Therapist

Start with Psychology Today Canada. Filter by "career/work issues" and "sliding scale" if cost is a concern. Read 2-3 profiles and book a free consultation. You don't have to commit to the first person you talk to.

- [Psychology Today Canada](https://www.psychologytoday.com/ca) — filter by issue, fee, insurance
- [CCPA Therapist Finder](https://www.ccpa-accp.ca) — Canadian Counselling Association
- [CRPO Directory](https://www.crpo.ca) — Ontario registered psychotherapists
- Check if your spouse's benefits cover therapy
- Some professional associations (e.g., PEng, CPA) offer member assistance programs. Check yours.
- Look for local job search networking groups or career transition meetups. Being around others going through the same thing helps.

## Weekly Check-In

Come back to these questions each week. Be honest with yourself.

- [ ] How am I sleeping this week?
- [ ] How many times did I leave the house?
- [ ] Did I talk to someone about how I'm actually feeling?
- [ ] Am I respecting my stop time, or doom-scrolling job boards at midnight?

Notice the trend, not the day. Bad days still happen at week 8. But compare this week to week 1.

## If You're Supporting Someone Who Was Laid Off

- Don't say "it'll be fine" or "you'll find something better." Just listen.
- Don't ask "how's the job search going?" every day. Let them bring it up.
- Keep normal routines (meals together, activities, social plans).
- Take care of your own stress too. This affects both of you. Your anxiety is valid, and ignoring it helps no one.
- Schedule one financial conversation per week instead of daily check-ins. Use the Financial Runway Worksheet to ground it in real numbers rather than fear.
- If they're withdrawing, not sleeping, or drinking more than usual for more than two weeks, gently suggest talking to a professional. You can offer to help find one. The resources in this guide are a good starting point.

---

## Key Takeaway

A layoff is one of the most stressful life events you can go through. What you're feeling is a normal response to an abnormal situation. It gets easier. Use the tools here. Talk to someone. You don't have to do this alone.

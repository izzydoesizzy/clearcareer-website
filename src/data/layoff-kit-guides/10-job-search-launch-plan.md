# 30/60/90 Day Job Search Launch Plan

**A structured day-by-day plan so you always know what to do next.**

> **Note:** Job search timelines vary by industry, role, and market conditions. This is a general framework, not a guarantee.

You don't need to do everything today. Work through this at your own pace.

**Time:** Work through this plan over 90 days. **What you'll need:** Your resume, LinkedIn profile, and the other guides in this kit.

**Top 3 actions from this guide:**
1. Tell one trusted person today. You need someone in your corner from day one.
2. Apply for EI at canada.ca/ei right now. Do not wait.
3. Write down your top 10 achievements while your memory is fresh. You will use these everywhere.

---

## Days 1-7: Foundation Week

**Goal: Handle the urgent stuff and set up your infrastructure.**

### Days 1-2: Administrative
- [ ] Tell one trusted person
- [ ] Request termination details in writing
- [ ] Do NOT sign the severance (use the Severance Negotiation Playbook)
- [ ] Save personal files before losing access. Do not copy company proprietary data, client lists, or confidential documents.
- [ ] Screenshot your benefits summary
- [ ] Apply for EI at [canada.ca/ei](https://www.canada.ca/en/services/benefits/ei/ei-regular-benefit/apply.html)

### Days 3-4: Financial
- [ ] Complete the Financial Runway Worksheet
- [ ] Review severance tax strategy (Tax Planning guide)
- [ ] Pause non-essential spending
- [ ] Set a job search budget ($200-$500 for coffee meetings, transit, courses)

### Days 5-7: Career Prep
- [ ] Write down your top 10 achievements while memory is fresh
- [ ] Research target salary (Glassdoor, Robert Half, Indeed, Hays) (see the Salary Negotiation Scripts guide for a deeper framework)
- [ ] List 5-8 job titles you'd consider
- [ ] Identify your top 20 target companies
- [ ] Review your non-compete (see Non-Compete Audit)

**Action:** Complete this checklist by end of day 7.

---

## Days 8-14: Profile Week

**Goal: Optimize your marketing materials before anyone sees them.**

### LinkedIn Overhaul (2-3 hours)
- [ ] Update headline (see LinkedIn Optimization guide)
- [ ] Rewrite About section using the 4-paragraph template
- [ ] Update experience bullets with metrics and impact
- [ ] Add 15+ relevant skills, pin top 3
- [ ] Set custom URL
- [ ] Update banner image
- [ ] Turn on "Open to Work" (recruiter-only mode)

### Resume Update (2-3 hours)
- [ ] Update with most recent role and achievements
- [ ] Every bullet needs a metric or result
- [ ] Create 2-3 versions for your top target roles
- [ ] Save as clean, ATS-friendly PDF
- [ ] Proofread. Then proofread again.

### Digital Footprint Check
- [ ] Google yourself. Clean up anything unprofessional.
- [ ] Review social media profiles
- [ ] Update email signature (remove former company)

**Action:** Your LinkedIn and resume should be ready before you start reaching out.

## Days 15-30: Outreach Month

**Goal: Activate your network and start generating conversations.**

### Week 3: Warm Network

Contact 20 people you already know. Not "I need a job." Genuine check-ins:

```
Hey [Name], I hope you're doing well. I wanted to let you know I'm making a career move and exploring [type of role] opportunities. I'd love to catch up and hear what you've been up to. Do you have 15-20 minutes this week or next?
```

**Target: 5 conversations per week.**

### Week 4: Strategic Networking

Expand beyond people you know:
- [ ] Join 2-3 LinkedIn groups or Slack communities in your field
- [ ] Attend 1-2 industry events or virtual meetups
- [ ] Reach out to 5 people at target companies (2nd-degree connections)
- [ ] Start posting on LinkedIn 1-2x per week

```
Hi [Name], I came across your profile while researching [company/industry]. I'm currently exploring [type of role] and would love to learn about your experience at [company]. Would you be open to a 15-minute informational chat?
```

| Time | Activity | Duration |
|------|----------|----------|
| 8:00 AM | Morning routine (exercise, shower, dress) | 1 hour |
| 9:00 AM | Job board check + apply to 2-3 roles | 1.5 hours |
| 10:30 AM | Networking outreach (emails, LinkedIn) | 1 hour |
| 11:30 AM | Skill development or industry reading | 30 min |
| 12:00 PM | Lunch. Go outside. | 1 hour |
| 1:00 PM | Networking calls / informational interviews | 1-2 hours |
| 3:00 PM | Follow-ups, thank-you emails, admin | 30 min |
| 3:30 PM | Done for the day. | -- |

Adjust times to your reality. The structure matters more than the specific hours.

**Total: 5-6 hours/day.** This is enough. More than that leads to burnout.

## Days 31-60: Momentum Phase

**Goal: Convert conversations into interviews.**

### Weekly Targets

| Activity | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Quality applications | 5-10 per week |
| Networking conversations | 4-5 per week |
| LinkedIn posts | 1-2 per week |
| Informational interviews | 2-3 per week |
| Follow-ups | Within 48 hours, always |

### Application Tiers

Stop applying to everything. Quality over quantity.

**Tier 1 (Dream roles):** 45-60 min per application. Custom cover letter. Network into the company. 30% of your applications.

**Tier 2 (Good fits):** 20-30 min. Tailored resume, brief cover letter. 50% of your applications.

**Tier 3 (Practice/backup):** 10-15 min. Apply quickly. 20% of your applications.

### When Interviews Start

- [ ] Research the company (10-K, news, Glassdoor, LinkedIn employee profiles)
- [ ] Prepare 8-10 STAR stories covering your key achievements
- [ ] Prepare 5-7 questions to ask the interviewer
- [ ] Practice your 60-second "Tell me about yourself"
- [ ] Prepare your layoff answer:

```
My role was eliminated as part of a company restructuring. It was disappointing because I really enjoyed the work, but it's given me the opportunity to be intentional about my next move. I'm specifically interested in [this role] because [genuine reason].
```

## Days 61-90: Acceleration Phase

**Goal: Close opportunities and negotiate offers.**

### If You're Getting Interviews
- [ ] Refine answers based on feedback
- [ ] Follow up within 24 hours of every interview
- [ ] Keep networking (don't stop because interviews are happening)
- [ ] Prepare your negotiation strategy (see Salary Negotiation Scripts)

See the Mental Health & Resilience guide for strategies on managing stress and relationships during your search.

### If You're NOT Getting Interviews

Time to reassess:
- [ ] Is your resume getting past ATS?
- [ ] Are you applying to the right level?
- [ ] Is your LinkedIn optimized?
- [ ] Are you networking enough? (Most jobs come through connections)
- [ ] Consider expanding your target (new industries, adjacent roles, different locations)

## The 90-Day Checkpoint

Review your numbers honestly:

| Metric | Your Number | Healthy Range |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| Applications submitted | | 30-75 |
| Networking conversations | | 30-50 |
| Phone screens | | 8-15 |
| Full interviews | | 3-8 |
| Offers | | 1-3 |

These ranges vary by industry, seniority, and market conditions.

If you're below the healthy range, the bottleneck is usually your materials (resume/LinkedIn), your targeting (wrong roles), or your networking (not enough conversations).

## Working With Recruiters

1. Identify 5-10 recruiters who specialize in your function/industry
2. Connect on LinkedIn:

```
Hi [Name], I'm a [title] with [X years] in [industry], currently exploring new opportunities. I see you specialize in [area]. I'd love to connect in case you come across roles that might be a fit.
```

3. Respond within 24 hours when they reach out
4. Be honest about salary expectations
5. Don't rely on recruiters alone. They represent a meaningful but limited subset of the market.

**Red flags:** They ask you to pay upfront, pressure you to accept, or won't disclose the company name.

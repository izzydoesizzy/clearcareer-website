# 30/60/90 Day Job Search Launch Plan

**A structured day-by-day plan for your first three months of job searching after a layoff.**

> **Note:** This plan provides a general framework. Job search timelines and outcomes vary significantly by industry, role, market conditions, and individual circumstances. No specific outcome is guaranteed.

Unstructured job searching leads to burnout, despair, and bad decisions. This plan gives you a daily and weekly framework so you always know what to do next.

---

## Days 1-7: Foundation Week

**Goal: Handle the urgent stuff and set up your infrastructure.**

### Day 1-2: Administrative
- [ ] Request termination details in writing
- [ ] Do NOT sign the severance (review with the Severance Negotiation Playbook)
- [ ] Save personal files and contacts before losing access
- [ ] Screenshot your benefits summary
- [ ] Apply for EI immediately at [canada.ca/ei](https://www.canada.ca/en/services/benefits/ei/ei-regular-benefit/apply.html)
- [ ] Tell one trusted person

### Day 3-4: Financial
- [ ] Complete the Financial Runway Worksheet
- [ ] Calculate your severance tax strategy (see Tax Planning guide)
- [ ] Pause non-essential spending
- [ ] Set up a job search expense budget ($200-$500 for coffee meetings, transit, professional development)

### Day 5-7: Career Preparation
- [ ] Write down your top 10 achievements (while memory is fresh)
- [ ] Research your target salary (Glassdoor, Robert Half, Indeed, Hays)
- [ ] Make a list of 5-8 job titles you'd consider
- [ ] Identify your top 20 target companies
- [ ] Review your non-compete and restrictive covenants (see Non-Compete Audit)

---

## Days 8-14: Profile Week

**Goal: Optimize your marketing materials before anyone sees them.**

### LinkedIn Overhaul (2-3 hours)
- [ ] Update headline (see LinkedIn Optimization guide for formulas)
- [ ] Rewrite About section (4-paragraph template)
- [ ] Update experience bullets with metrics and impact
- [ ] Add 15+ relevant skills, pin top 3
- [ ] Set custom URL
- [ ] Update banner image
- [ ] Turn on "Open to Work" (recruiter-only mode)

### Resume Update (2-3 hours)
- [ ] Update with your most recent role and achievements
- [ ] Ensure every bullet has a metric or result
- [ ] Create 2-3 versions tailored to your top target roles
- [ ] Save as PDF with a clean, ATS-friendly format
- [ ] Proofread. Then proofread again.

### Digital Footprint Check
- [ ] Google yourself. Clean up anything unprofessional.
- [ ] Review social media profiles for anything that could raise flags
- [ ] Update your email signature (remove former company references)

## Days 15-30: Outreach Month

**Goal: Activate your network and start generating conversations.**

### Week 3: Warm Network Activation

Contact your list of 20 people. Not with "I need a job." With genuine check-ins:

**Script for warm outreach:**
> "Hey [Name], I hope you're doing well. I wanted to let you know I'm making a career move and exploring [type of role] opportunities. I'd love to catch up and hear what you've been up to. Do you have 15-20 minutes this week or next?"

**Target: 5 conversations per week**

### Week 4: Strategic Networking

Expand beyond your warm network:

- [ ] Join 2-3 relevant LinkedIn groups or Slack communities
- [ ] Attend 1-2 industry events or virtual meetups
- [ ] Reach out to 5 people at your target companies (2nd-degree connections)
- [ ] Start posting on LinkedIn (1-2x/week, industry insights or lessons learned)

**Networking script for strangers:**
> "Hi [Name], I came across your profile while researching [company/industry]. I'm currently exploring [type of role] and would love to learn about your experience at [company]. Would you be open to a 15-minute informational chat?"

## Daily Structure (Days 15-90)

| Time | Activity | Duration |
|------|----------|----------|
| 8:00 AM | Morning routine (exercise, shower, dress) | 1 hour |
| 9:00 AM | Job board check + apply to 2-3 roles | 1.5 hours |
| 10:30 AM | Networking outreach (emails, LinkedIn messages) | 1 hour |
| 11:30 AM | Skill development or industry reading | 30 min |
| 12:00 PM | Lunch. Go outside. | 1 hour |
| 1:00 PM | Networking calls / informational interviews | 1-2 hours |
| 3:00 PM | Follow-ups, thank-you emails, admin | 30 min |
| 3:30 PM | Done for the day. | — |

**Total active job search: 5-6 hours/day.** This is enough. More than that leads to diminishing returns and burnout.

## Days 31-60: Momentum Phase

**Goal: Convert conversations into interviews and applications into callbacks.**

### Weekly Targets

| Activity | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Applications submitted | 5-10 quality applications |
| Networking conversations | 4-5 per week |
| LinkedIn posts | 1-2 per week |
| Informational interviews | 2-3 per week |
| Follow-up emails | All outstanding within 48 hours |

### Application Strategy

**Stop applying to everything.** Quality over quantity.

1. **Tier 1 (Dream roles):** Roles at target companies that match your experience. Spend 45-60 minutes per application. Custom cover letter. Network into the company.
2. **Tier 2 (Good fits):** Roles that match your skills but aren't your top picks. Spend 20-30 minutes. Tailored resume, brief cover letter.
3. **Tier 3 (Practice/backup):** Roles you could do but wouldn't love. Spend 10-15 minutes. Apply quickly.

**Ratio: 30% Tier 1, 50% Tier 2, 20% Tier 3**

### Interview Prep

When interviews start coming in:
- [ ] Research the company thoroughly (10-K, news, Glassdoor reviews, LinkedIn employee profiles)
- [ ] Prepare 8-10 STAR stories (Situation, Task, Action, Result) covering your key achievements
- [ ] Prepare 5-7 thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer
- [ ] Practice answering "Tell me about yourself" (30-60 second version)
- [ ] Prepare your "Why did you leave?" answer:

> "My role was eliminated as part of a company restructuring. It was disappointing because I really enjoyed the work, but it's given me the opportunity to be very intentional about my next move. I'm specifically interested in [this role] because [genuine reason]."

## Days 61-90: Acceleration Phase

**Goal: Close opportunities and negotiate offers.**

### If You're Getting Interviews

You're on track. Focus on:
- [ ] Refining your interview answers based on feedback
- [ ] Following up within 24 hours of every interview
- [ ] Continuing to network (don't stop because you have interviews)
- [ ] Preparing your negotiation strategy (see Salary Negotiation Scripts)

### If You're NOT Getting Interviews

It's time to reassess:
- [ ] Review your resume. Is it getting past ATS (applicant tracking systems)?
- [ ] Are you applying to the right level of roles?
- [ ] Is your LinkedIn profile optimized?
- [ ] Are you networking enough? (Most jobs come through connections, not applications)
- [ ] Consider expanding your target (new industries, adjacent roles, different geographies)
- [ ] Consider working with a career coach for an objective assessment

### The 90-Day Checkpoint

At day 90, review honestly:

| Metric | Your Number | Healthy Range |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| Applications submitted | | 30-75 |
| Networking conversations | | 30-50 |
| Phone screens | | 8-15 |
| Interviews (full loop) | | 3-8 |
| Offers | | 1-3 |

If your numbers are significantly below the healthy range, the bottleneck is usually either your materials (resume/LinkedIn), your targeting (wrong roles/companies), or your networking (not enough conversations). Identify which one and focus there.

## Recruiter Outreach Strategy

### How to work with recruiters:
1. **Identify 5-10 recruiters** who specialize in your function/industry
2. **Connect on LinkedIn** with a brief message:
   > "Hi [Name], I'm a [title] with [X years] in [industry], currently exploring new opportunities. I see you specialize in [area]. I'd love to connect in case you come across roles that might be a fit."
3. **Be responsive.** When a recruiter reaches out, respond within 24 hours.
4. **Be honest about your salary expectations.** Recruiters work on commission. They want to place you at the highest number possible.
5. **Don't rely solely on recruiters.** They represent 15-20% of the job market. Your own networking is the primary engine.

### Red flags in recruiter relationships:
- They ask you to pay upfront (legitimate recruiters are paid by employers)
- They pressure you to accept a role you're not sure about
- They won't tell you the company name (reasonable for confidential searches, but you should know before interviewing)
- They ghost you after an interview (follow up, then move on)

## Key Takeaway

A job search is a project. Like any project, it needs structure, targets, and regular checkpoints. The plan above gives you exactly that. Following a structured approach tends to produce better outcomes than refreshing job boards without a plan. Consistency beats intensity. Show up every day, do the work, and the results will come.

# 90-Day Transition Plan

**Month-by-month actions to move from "I want to change careers" to "I start Monday."**

You have done the exploration. You know what you want. You have the financial runway. Now you need a plan that turns intention into action across three months.

This is not a rigid schedule. Life happens. But having a month-by-month framework keeps you from spinning your wheels, and it gives you milestones to measure progress against.

**Time:** 20 minutes to customize the plan. Then follow it for 90 days. **What you'll need:** Everything you have built in Guides 01-06 of this series.

---

## Month 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)

Month 1 is about building the infrastructure for your career change. You are not applying to jobs yet. You are getting ready.

### Week 1: Lock Down the Target

- [ ] Finalize your target role(s). Pick 2-3 specific job titles.
- [ ] Write your career change statement in one sentence: "I am transitioning from [OLD FIELD] to [NEW FIELD] because [REASON], bringing [X YEARS] of experience in [TRANSFERABLE SKILLS]."
- [ ] Identify 20-30 target companies. Research each one (Guide 05).
- [ ] Set up your tracking system (spreadsheet or CRM, Guide 04).

### Week 2: Close the Gaps

- [ ] Complete your skills gap analysis (Guide 02). List your 3-5 must-close gaps.
- [ ] Enroll in a course, certification, or self-study program for your biggest gap.
- [ ] Start building a portfolio project if your target field values portfolios (design, writing, development, marketing).
- [ ] Start your industry research (Guide 05, Week 1).

### Week 3: Build the Materials

- [ ] Rewrite your resume in the functional-hybrid format (Guide 06).
- [ ] Rewrite your LinkedIn profile for the target role (headline, about, experience).
- [ ] Draft your cover letter using one of the three variants (Guide 06).
- [ ] Complete your industry research (Guide 05, Week 2).

### Week 4: Activate the Network

- [ ] Build your initial contact list of 30+ people (Guide 04).
- [ ] Send 10 reconnection messages to warm contacts.
- [ ] Send 5 cold outreach messages to people in your target industry.
- [ ] Schedule 3-5 informational interviews for Month 2.
- [ ] Post on LinkedIn about your career exploration (not a full announcement, just engagement with industry content).

### Month 1 Milestones

| Milestone | Target | Done? |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Target role defined | 2-3 specific titles | [ ] |
| Target companies listed | 20-30 companies | [ ] |
| Resume rewritten for target field | Complete | [ ] |
| LinkedIn optimized | Complete | [ ] |
| Skill-building started | Course enrolled or project begun | [ ] |
| Network outreach begun | 15+ messages sent | [ ] |
| Informational interviews scheduled | 3-5 scheduled | [ ] |

## Month 2: Build and Connect (Days 31-60)

Month 2 is about deepening your knowledge, expanding your network, and starting to create proof that you belong in your new field.

### Week 5: Informational Interviews

- [ ] Conduct 3-5 informational interviews. Take notes. Send thank-you messages within 24 hours.
- [ ] After each conversation, update your target list and approach based on what you learned.
- [ ] Ask every person: "Who else should I be talking to?" Follow up on every introduction.
- [ ] Continue skill-building (course work, portfolio project, practice).

### Week 6: Build Credibility

- [ ] Publish 1-2 LinkedIn posts related to your target industry. Share a learning, an insight, or a resource.
- [ ] Comment thoughtfully on 5-10 industry posts per week.
- [ ] Attend 1 industry event, webinar, or meetup (virtual or in-person).
- [ ] Continue sending 5-10 networking messages per week.

### Week 7: Start Applying (Selectively)

- [ ] Identify 5-10 job postings that match your target and where you meet 80%+ of qualifications.
- [ ] Customize your resume and cover letter for each one.
- [ ] Where possible, reach out to someone at the company before or alongside your application.
- [ ] Continue skill-building and networking simultaneously.

### Week 8: Assess and Adjust

- [ ] Review your Month 1-2 activity. What is working? What is not?
- [ ] Are you getting responses to outreach? If not, revise your messaging.
- [ ] Are you getting interviews? If not, review your resume with someone in the target field.
- [ ] Are your informational interviews leading to referrals? If not, ask more directly.
- [ ] Adjust your target list based on what you have learned (add companies, remove others).

### Month 2 Milestones

| Milestone | Target | Done? |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Informational interviews completed | 5-8 total | [ ] |
| LinkedIn presence active | Posting weekly, commenting daily | [ ] |
| Applications submitted | 5-10 quality applications | [ ] |
| Industry event attended | At least 1 | [ ] |
| Skill gap progress | Course/certification 50%+ complete | [ ] |
| Network size | 50+ meaningful connections | [ ] |

## Month 3: Push and Close (Days 61-90)

Month 3 is about maximum effort on applications, interviews, and converting your network into opportunities.

### Week 9-10: Application Sprint

- [ ] Submit 10-15 quality applications (customized, not mass-applied).
- [ ] For each application, reach out to someone at the company (hiring manager, recruiter, or team member).
- [ ] Follow up on all outstanding applications from Month 2.
- [ ] Continue networking outreach: 10+ messages per week.
- [ ] Complete your skill-building milestone (course, certification, or portfolio piece).

### Week 11: Interview Preparation

- [ ] Build your STAR story bank for the new industry (adapt your stories using target industry language).
- [ ] Practice your "Tell me about yourself" answer with the career change narrative.
- [ ] Prepare for "Why the career change?" question (1-2 sentences, forward-looking).
- [ ] Do 2-3 mock interviews with someone in the target field.
- [ ] Prepare questions to ask interviewers that demonstrate industry knowledge.

### Week 12: Evaluate and Plan Forward

- [ ] Review your entire 90-day journey. What worked? What would you change?
- [ ] If you have interviews or offers in progress, focus energy there.
- [ ] If you do not have traction, diagnose the problem (see troubleshooting below).
- [ ] Set your next 30-day plan based on your current position.

### Month 3 Milestones

| Milestone | Target | Done? |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Total quality applications | 20-30 | [ ] |
| Interviews secured | 3-5 | [ ] |
| Skill gap closed | Course/certification complete | [ ] |
| Portfolio or proof of work | At least 1 completed project | [ ] |
| Network | 75+ meaningful connections | [ ] |
| Referrals received | 3-5 | [ ] |

## Contingency Plan

Not everyone lands a new career in 90 days. If you reach Day 90 without an offer, here is how to diagnose and adjust.

### Problem: Not Getting Interviews

| Possible Cause | Fix |
|---------------|-----|
| Resume not resonating | Get it reviewed by someone who hires for your target role |
| Applying to wrong-level roles | Adjust up or down based on your experience |
| Not enough applications | Increase volume to 5-10 per week |
| No referrals or warm introductions | Invest more in networking, less in cold applications |
| Skills gap is bigger than you thought | Prioritize skill-building before applying more |

### Problem: Getting Interviews but No Offers

| Possible Cause | Fix |
|---------------|-----|
| Interview skills need work | More mock interviews, record yourself, get feedback |
| Career change narrative is not clear | Practice your "Why the change?" answer until it is crisp |
| Not demonstrating industry knowledge | More research, more informed questions |
| Not competitive on specific skills | Identify what you are losing on and close that gap |
| Targeting roles that are too senior | Apply to one level down and build from there |

### Problem: Not Getting Responses to Networking

| Possible Cause | Fix |
|---------------|-----|
| Messages too generic | Personalize every message. Reference something specific. |
| Asking for too much too soon | Ask for 15 minutes, not "help me find a job" |
| Not following up | Send a follow-up after 5-7 days |
| Wrong targets | Focus on people 1-2 levels above your target role, not executives |

## Key Takeaway

A 90-day career transition is ambitious but achievable if you follow the structure: foundation in Month 1, connection in Month 2, push in Month 3. The plan is not magic. It is focused, consistent effort across three dimensions (skills, network, and applications) over a defined time period.

Customize the plan to your situation. Hit the milestones. Adjust when things do not work. And remember: even if it takes 120 or 150 days instead of 90, you are still moving forward.

The people who successfully change careers are not luckier than you. They are more systematic. Now you have the system.

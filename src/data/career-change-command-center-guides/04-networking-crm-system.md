# Networking CRM System

**Build a professional network from zero. No schmoozing required.**

Networking does not mean working a room with a stack of business cards and a fake smile. That is the version of networking that makes introverts and career changers break out in a cold sweat. Real networking is simpler: have genuine conversations with people, keep track of those conversations, and follow up consistently.

This guide helps you build a system for managing relationships, even if your current professional network is basically empty.

**Time:** 30 minutes to set up your system. 15 minutes per week to maintain it. **What you'll need:** A spreadsheet (Google Sheets, Excel, or Notion) and a willingness to reach out to people.

---

## Step 1: Build Your Contact List

You have more contacts than you think. Start by listing everyone you know, then categorize them.

### Category 1: Inner Circle (5-15 people)
People who would take your call right now. Close friends, family, former colleagues you are still in touch with, mentors, classmates you actually talk to.

### Category 2: Warm Contacts (15-50 people)
People who know your name but you have not spoken to recently. Former colleagues, LinkedIn connections you have actually met, people from previous jobs, alumni network, neighbors, parents of your kids' friends.

### Category 3: Target Contacts (unlimited)
People you do not know yet but want to meet. Employees at target companies, people in your target industry, hiring managers, industry thought leaders, people whose career path you admire.

### The Contact CRM Template

Create a spreadsheet with these columns:

| Column | What to Track |
|--------|--------------|
| Name | Full name |
| Category | Inner Circle / Warm / Target |
| Company | Where they work |
| Role | Their job title |
| Connection | How you know them (or how you will reach out) |
| Email | If you have it |
| LinkedIn | Profile URL |
| Last Contact | Date of last meaningful interaction |
| Next Action | What to do next and when |
| Notes | Key details (what they care about, family info, interests) |

Start by entering everyone from Categories 1 and 2. Then add 10-20 people from Category 3 to start.

## Step 2: The Outreach Cadence

### For Inner Circle (Monthly)
- Check in once a month. Not "are you hiring?" but a genuine connection.
- Tell them what you are looking for, specifically. "I'm exploring roles in [X] at companies like [Y] and [Z]."
- Ask: "Who in your network should I be talking to?"

### For Warm Contacts (Quarterly)
- Reconnect every 2-3 months with a reason (share an article, congratulate them on something, ask a genuine question).
- After re-establishing the connection, mention your career change.
- Ask for introductions to people in your target field.

### For Target Contacts (Once, with follow-up)
- Cold outreach with a personalized message (see the Cold Outreach Scripts pack).
- Follow up once after 5-7 days if no response.
- If they respond, ask for a 15-minute conversation.
- After the conversation, move them to "Warm Contacts" and follow the quarterly cadence.

### The Weekly Networking Routine

| Day | Activity | Time |
|-----|----------|------|
| Monday | Review your CRM. Who needs a follow-up this week? | 10 min |
| Tuesday | Send 3-5 messages (reconnections, follow-ups, cold outreach) | 15 min |
| Thursday | Send 3-5 more messages | 15 min |
| Friday | Update your CRM with any responses, notes, and next actions | 10 min |

Total weekly time: about 50 minutes. That is it. Consistency matters more than volume.

## Step 3: 20 Conversation Starters

The hardest part of networking is knowing what to say. Here are 20 openers for different situations.

### Reconnecting with Warm Contacts (5)

1. "Hey [NAME], it's been a while! I saw your post about [TOPIC] and it made me think of you. How have things been?"

2. "Hi [NAME], I just saw that [COMPANY] announced [NEWS]. That must be exciting. How are things going on your end?"

3. "[NAME]! I was cleaning up my LinkedIn and realized we haven't connected in ages. What are you working on these days?"

4. "Hi [NAME], I came across [ARTICLE/RESOURCE] and thought of you because of your work in [AREA]. Thought you might find it interesting."

5. "Hey [NAME], I'm making a career shift into [FIELD] and you're one of the first people I thought of. Would love to catch up and get your perspective."

### Cold Outreach to Target Contacts (5)

6. "Hi [NAME], I'm exploring a move into [INDUSTRY] and your career path is exactly the kind of trajectory I'm aiming for. Would you have 15 minutes for a conversation?"

7. "Hi [NAME], I found your profile while researching [COMPANY]. Your work in [AREA] is impressive. Would you be open to a quick chat about what it's like working there?"

8. "[NAME], your post about [TOPIC] was one of the most useful things I've read this month. I'm transitioning into [FIELD] and would love to ask you a few questions if you have a few minutes."

9. "Hi [NAME], [MUTUAL CONNECTION] suggested I reach out to you. I'm a [YOUR BACKGROUND] exploring opportunities in [AREA]. Would you have time for a brief conversation?"

10. "Hi [NAME], I notice we both [SHARED TRAIT: went to the same school, are in the same group, worked at the same company]. I'm exploring [FIELD] and would value your perspective."

### During Informational Interviews (5)

11. "What does a typical week look like in your role?"

12. "What do you wish you had known before entering this field?"

13. "What skills or experiences do you think are most important for someone breaking into this industry?"

14. "What is the biggest challenge you face in your role?"

15. "If you were starting over today and trying to break into this field, what would you do first?"

### Follow-Up After Conversations (5)

16. "Thank you for the conversation today. Your point about [SPECIFIC THING] was really helpful. I'm going to follow up on [SPECIFIC ACTION THEY SUGGESTED]."

17. "Great chatting with you. I wanted to share [ARTICLE/RESOURCE] that relates to what we discussed about [TOPIC]."

18. "Quick update: I followed your advice about [THING] and [RESULT]. Thank you for pointing me in the right direction."

19. "Hi [NAME], I just applied for the [ROLE] at [COMPANY] that you mentioned. Thank you for the tip. I'll let you know how it goes."

20. "Hey [NAME], I've been thinking about what you said regarding [TOPIC]. Would you be open to continuing that conversation over coffee/a call?"

## Step 4: Networking Rules

### Do

- **Be specific about what you are looking for.** "I'm looking for product marketing roles at mid-stage B2B SaaS companies" is useful. "I'm looking for something in tech" is not.
- **Give before you ask.** Share articles, make introductions, offer help. The best networkers are givers, not takers.
- **Follow up within 24 hours.** After every conversation, send a thank-you message. This alone puts you ahead of 90% of people.
- **Keep notes.** Write down what you talked about so you can reference it next time. People are impressed when you remember details.
- **Be patient.** Networking ROI is not immediate. It compounds over weeks and months.

### Do Not

- **Do not ask for a job in the first conversation.** Ask for advice, perspective, and introductions. Jobs come later.
- **Do not network only when you need something.** Build relationships during good times too.
- **Do not mass-message.** Every message should be personalized to the recipient.
- **Do not apologize for reaching out.** "Sorry to bother you" undercuts your message. You are not a bother. You are a professional having a professional conversation.
- **Do not disappear after someone helps you.** Follow up with updates. People who help you want to know that their help mattered.

## Step 5: The Networking Funnel

Think of networking like a funnel. Most contacts will not turn into job leads. That is by design.

| Stage | Volume | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Connection requests sent | 20-30/month | Build your network |
| Conversations had | 5-10/month | Learn and build relationships |
| Referrals received | 1-3/month | Get warm introductions |
| Interviews from referrals | 1-2/month | Turn relationships into opportunities |

You need 20-30 connection attempts to get 5-10 conversations, which yield 1-3 referrals, which lead to 1-2 interviews. This math is consistent and predictable. Trust the numbers.

---

## Key Takeaway

Networking is not a talent. It is a system. Build the CRM. Follow the cadence. Send the messages. The results will come, not from any single conversation, but from the compound effect of showing up consistently.

You do not need to be charming. You need to be genuine, consistent, and organized. The CRM handles the organized part. You handle the rest.

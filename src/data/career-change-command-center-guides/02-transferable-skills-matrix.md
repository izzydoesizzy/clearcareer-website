# Transferable Skills Matrix

**You already have 80% of what your next career requires. This guide helps you find it.**

Career changers consistently underestimate how much of their existing skill set transfers to a new field. They look at job postings, see unfamiliar terminology, and assume they are starting from scratch. They are not.

This guide walks you through an 8-category skills audit, helps you identify genuine gaps (they are smaller than you think), and introduces the "80% Ready" rule that will stop you from over-preparing and start you applying.

**Time:** 45-60 minutes for the full audit. **What you'll need:** Your resume, 5-10 job postings in your target field, and a highlighter.

## The 8-Category Skills Audit

Most people think in narrow skill categories: "I know Excel" or "I can manage people." But skills exist across eight broader categories. When you audit yourself across all eight, you discover transferable skills you never would have listed on a resume.

## The "80% Ready" Rule

Here is the rule that will change your career transition: **if you have 80% of the skills listed in a job posting, you are qualified to apply.**

Job postings are wish lists, not checklists. Companies write them describing the ideal candidate, knowing that person may not exist. Research consistently shows that men apply to jobs when they meet 60% of the qualifications, while women wait until they meet 100%. The result is not that under-qualified men get hired. The result is that the 80% candidates (who could do the job well) are sitting on the sidelines.

The remaining 20% is what you learn in the first 3-6 months on the job. Every single employee in history has had a learning curve. That is expected.

### How to assess your readiness:

1. Count the total requirements in the job posting (including "nice to haves")
2. Count how many you meet (including close equivalents)
3. Divide: your count / total = your percentage

Example: 15 requirements listed. You meet 12. That is 80%. Apply.

If you are at 60-79%, apply anyway but emphasize your transferable skills strongly in the cover letter.

If you are below 60%, this role might be a stretch right now. Focus on building the missing skills first.

## The Translation Cheat Sheet

Every industry has its own vocabulary. Same skills, different words. Here are common translations:

| General / Previous Industry | Tech | Healthcare | Finance | Marketing |
|----------------------------|------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Project management | Agile/Scrum | Care coordination | Deal management | Campaign management |
| Customer service | Customer success | Patient experience | Client relations | Community management |
| Data analysis | Analytics | Clinical data | Financial modeling | Marketing analytics |
| Process improvement | DevOps / CI/CD | Quality improvement | Operational efficiency | Conversion optimization |
| Training | Enablement | Clinical education | Compliance training | Content strategy |
| Reporting | Dashboards / BI | Outcomes reporting | Regulatory reporting | Performance reporting |

**Your job:** learn the vocabulary of your target industry. Read their job postings, their blogs, their LinkedIn posts. Use their words on your resume, even if you learned the skill under a different name.

---

## Key Takeaway

You are not starting from zero. You are translating. The skills you have spent years building are valuable in more contexts than you realize. Audit what you have, identify the small number of genuine gaps, and stop waiting until you feel 100% ready.

Eighty percent is enough. Start applying.

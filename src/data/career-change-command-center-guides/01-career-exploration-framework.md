# Career Exploration Framework

**Figure out what you actually want before you start chasing it.**

Most career changers skip this step. They jump straight from "I hate my job" to "I'll be a UX designer" without ever asking whether UX design is genuinely right for them or just the trendy option that showed up in their feed. Six months and a $5,000 bootcamp later, they realize the day-to-day work is not what they imagined.

This guide helps you explore first and decide second. You will clarify your values, map your skills, and use a creative exercise to surface interests you did not know you had.

**Time:** 60-90 minutes, ideally across two sittings. **What you'll need:** A quiet space, a pen, and the willingness to think honestly about what matters to you.

## Step 3: The "10 Lives" Exercise

This exercise comes from the book "Designing Your Life" by Bill Burnett and Dave Evans. It is one of the most effective career exploration tools ever created.

**The prompt:** If you could live 10 parallel lives, what would you do in each one? There are no constraints. Money is not an issue. Education is not an issue. Age is not an issue. What 10 completely different lives would you live?

## Step 5: Test Before You Commit

Do not quit your job based on this framework alone. Test your top options in the real world first.

### Low-Risk Testing Methods

| Test | Time Investment | What You Learn |
|------|----------------|---------------|
| **Informational interviews** (3-5 per option) | 5-10 hours | What the daily work actually looks like |
| **Job shadowing** (1 day) | 8 hours | Whether the environment suits you |
| **Freelancing or volunteering** (1 project) | 10-20 hours | Whether you enjoy doing the work, not just thinking about it |
| **Online course** (intro level) | 5-15 hours | Whether the subject holds your interest past the initial excitement |
| **Industry events or meetups** (2-3) | 3-6 hours | Whether you connect with the people in this field |

The goal of testing is to answer one question: "Do I enjoy doing this work, or do I just enjoy the idea of doing this work?"

Those are two very different things.

---

## Key Takeaway

Career exploration is not navel-gazing. It is research. The time you invest here prevents expensive mistakes later: wrong bootcamps, wrong industries, wrong roles. Clarity about what you want is the single most valuable asset in a career change.

Do the exercises. Test the options. Then move with confidence.

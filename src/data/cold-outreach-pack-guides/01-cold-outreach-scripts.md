# Cold Outreach Scripts

**30 copy-paste scripts for reaching out to strangers who can help you get hired.**

Cold outreach works. Not every time, not with every person, but consistently enough that it should be a core part of your job search. The problem is that most people either never do it (too scary) or do it badly (generic messages that get ignored).

This guide gives you 30 scripts you can customize and send today. Every script has [BRACKET] fields for you to fill in with your specific details. Do not send them without customizing. A personalized cold message gets 3-5x the response rate of a generic one.

**Time:** 10 minutes to customize each script. **What you'll need:** Names of people you want to reach, a bit of research on each one, and your resume nearby for reference.

---

## Cold Emails (5 Scripts)

These work for reaching out to hiring managers, team leads, or anyone at a target company when you have their email address.

### Email 1: The Mutual Interest

**Subject:** [SHARED INTEREST/TOPIC] + [THEIR COMPANY NAME]

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I came across your [POST/TALK/ARTICLE] about [SPECIFIC TOPIC] and it got me thinking about [YOUR BRIEF INSIGHT OR REACTION, 1-2 sentences].

I'm a [YOUR TITLE/SPECIALTY] with [X YEARS] of experience in [YOUR AREA]. I've been following [COMPANY NAME] for a while, particularly [SOMETHING SPECIFIC YOU ADMIRE ABOUT THE COMPANY].

I'd love to learn more about what your team is working on. Would you have 15 minutes for a quick call sometime in the next couple of weeks?

Either way, thanks for sharing your work on [TOPIC].

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR LINKEDIN URL]
```

### Email 2: The Specific Value Add

**Subject:** Idea for [THEIR TEAM/PROJECT/INITIATIVE]

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I noticed [COMPANY NAME] is [SPECIFIC THING: launching a new product, expanding into a market, hiring for a team]. I've spent the last [X YEARS] doing exactly this kind of work at [YOUR PREVIOUS COMPANY/COMPANIES].

One thing I've seen work well in this space is [BRIEF, SPECIFIC INSIGHT that demonstrates your expertise, 1-2 sentences]. Happy to share more if it's useful.

I'm exploring new opportunities right now and [COMPANY NAME] is at the top of my list. If your team has capacity for a conversation, I'd welcome the chance to connect.

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR LINKEDIN URL]
```

### Email 3: The Referral Ask

**Subject:** [MUTUAL CONNECTION'S NAME] suggested I reach out

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

[MUTUAL CONNECTION'S NAME] mentioned you'd be a great person to talk to about [TOPIC/INDUSTRY/ROLE TYPE]. I'm a [YOUR TITLE] with experience in [YOUR SPECIALTY], and I'm currently exploring opportunities in [INDUSTRY/FUNCTION].

I'm not asking you to help me get a job. I'd just appreciate 15 minutes of your perspective on [SPECIFIC QUESTION ABOUT THE INDUSTRY/COMPANY/ROLE].

Would you have time for a brief call in the next week or two? I'm flexible on timing.

Thank you,
[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR LINKEDIN URL]
```

### Email 4: The Company Fan

**Subject:** Quick question about [COMPANY NAME]

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I've been following [COMPANY NAME] since [SPECIFIC MOMENT: product launch, news article, industry event]. What you're doing with [SPECIFIC INITIATIVE] really stands out in the [INDUSTRY] space.

I'm a [YOUR TITLE] with [X YEARS] in [YOUR AREA], and I'm exploring my next move. I'd love to understand what it's like working at [COMPANY NAME] from someone on the inside.

Would you be open to a 15-minute conversation? No pressure at all. I know cold emails are a lot.

Thanks either way,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Email 5: The Direct Ask

**Subject:** [YOUR TITLE] interested in [COMPANY NAME]

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I'll keep this short. I'm a [YOUR TITLE] with [X YEARS] of experience in [YOUR SPECIALTY]. I'm looking for my next role, and [COMPANY NAME] is a company I'd be excited to join.

My background:
- [KEY ACHIEVEMENT 1, with a number if possible]
- [KEY ACHIEVEMENT 2, with a number if possible]
- [KEY ACHIEVEMENT 3, with a number if possible]

I saw [COMPANY NAME] has [OPEN ROLE / is growing the team / is doing interesting work in AREA]. Would it make sense to connect?

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR LINKEDIN URL]
[YOUR PHONE NUMBER, optional]
```

## LinkedIn Connection Requests (5 Scripts)

LinkedIn gives you 300 characters for a connection note. Every character counts. Never send a blank request.

### Request 1: The Shared Background

```
Hi [FIRST NAME], I'm a fellow [SHARED TRAIT: alumni, industry, city, role type]. I've been following [COMPANY NAME]'s work in [AREA] and would love to connect. I'm a [YOUR TITLE] exploring opportunities in [SPACE]. Hope to stay in touch!
```

### Request 2: The Content Compliment

```
Hi [FIRST NAME], your recent post about [SPECIFIC TOPIC] really resonated. I work in [YOUR AREA] and see the same thing. Would love to connect and follow more of your thinking on [TOPIC].
```

### Request 3: The Recruiter Connect

```
Hi [FIRST NAME], I see you recruit for [FUNCTION/INDUSTRY] roles. I'm a [YOUR TITLE] with [X YEARS] in [YOUR SPECIALTY], actively exploring new opportunities. Would love to be on your radar. Happy to share my resume if useful.
```

### Request 4: The Company Target

```
Hi [FIRST NAME], I'm researching [COMPANY NAME] as a potential next step in my career. Your background in [THEIR ROLE/DEPARTMENT] caught my eye. Would you be open to a brief conversation about your experience there?
```

### Request 5: The Event Follow-Up

```
Hi [FIRST NAME], great meeting you at [EVENT/WEBINAR/CONFERENCE]. I enjoyed your perspective on [SPECIFIC TOPIC]. Would love to stay connected and continue the conversation.
```

## Follow-Up Cadence (3-Touch System)

Most people give up after one message. The data says follow-ups are where the magic happens. 80% of deals close after the 5th contact. Your 3-touch cadence keeps you persistent without being annoying.

### Touch 1: Initial Outreach (Day 0)

Send your cold email or LinkedIn request using one of the scripts above.

### Touch 2: The Gentle Bump (Day 5-7)

If no response, send this follow-up:

**Email version:**
```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Just floating this back to the top of your inbox. I know things get busy. If you have a few minutes to connect about [ORIGINAL TOPIC/ASK], I'd really appreciate it. If not, no worries at all.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**LinkedIn message version:**
```
Hi [FIRST NAME], just wanted to follow up on my earlier message. I know LinkedIn can be a firehose. If you have a few minutes to chat about [TOPIC], I'd welcome the conversation. Either way, thanks for connecting!
```

### Touch 3: The Value Add (Day 12-14)

If still no response, provide something useful instead of just asking again:

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Circling back one last time. I came across [ARTICLE/REPORT/NEWS] about [TOPIC RELEVANT TO THEM] and thought of our conversation. [ONE SENTENCE about why it's relevant to them.]

[LINK]

If you ever have a few minutes to connect, I'm around. Wishing you a great [WEEK/MONTH].

[YOUR NAME]
```

**After 3 touches with no response, move on.** Do not send a 4th. Some people just don't respond to cold outreach. That is fine. Focus your energy on people who engage.

## Referral Requests (5 Scripts)

Referrals are the single most effective way to get hired. These scripts help you ask for them without being awkward.

### Referral 1: The Direct Ask (for close contacts)

```
Hey [FIRST NAME],

I'm looking for my next role in [YOUR SPECIALTY], and I noticed [COMPANY NAME] has a [ROLE TITLE] opening. I know you work there / know someone there. Would you be comfortable referring me or making an introduction?

I've attached my resume for context. Happy to send you a summary of why I'd be a good fit if that would help.

Thanks, [YOUR NAME]
```

### Referral 2: The Soft Ask (for acquaintances)

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Hope you're doing well. I'm currently exploring new opportunities in [YOUR AREA/INDUSTRY]. I remembered you have connections in [SPACE/COMPANY] and wanted to ask: is there anyone in your network who might be worth talking to?

No pressure at all. Even a name or two would be a huge help. I'm happy to reach out cold and won't mention you unless you're comfortable with it.

Thanks, [YOUR NAME]
```

### Referral 3: The Alumni Network Ask

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Fellow [SCHOOL/PROGRAM] alum here. I'm a [YOUR TITLE] with [X YEARS] in [YOUR FIELD], and I'm exploring opportunities at [COMPANY NAME]. I noticed you've been there for [TIME PERIOD].

Would you be open to a quick chat about your experience? And if there's a fit, would you be comfortable passing along my resume to the right person?

Appreciate it either way.
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Referral 4: The Former Colleague Ask

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

It's been a while! Hope [COMPANY/LIFE UPDATE if you know one] is going well.

I'm on the market for a new [ROLE TYPE] role and I'm being intentional about where I land. Given our time working together at [PREVIOUS COMPANY], I was wondering if you'd be willing to be a reference and/or if you know anyone who's hiring for [ROLE TYPE] roles right now.

No pressure. Would love to catch up either way.
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Referral 5: The Informational-to-Referral Pivot

Use this after you have already had a conversation with someone at a target company:

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Thanks again for taking the time to chat last [DAY/WEEK]. I learned a lot about [SPECIFIC THING YOU DISCUSSED], and I'm even more excited about [COMPANY NAME].

I noticed the [ROLE TITLE] opening on your careers page. Based on our conversation, it sounds like it could be a strong fit. Would you be comfortable referring me through your internal system? Happy to send you whatever materials would help.

Thanks for all your help so far.
[YOUR NAME]
```

## Thank You Notes (5 Scripts)

Send these within 24 hours. Not 48. Not "later this week." Within 24 hours.

### Thank You 1: After an Informational Chat

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. Your insights about [SPECIFIC TOPIC DISCUSSED] were really helpful, especially [ONE SPECIFIC THING THEY SAID THAT STUCK WITH YOU].

I'll be following up on your suggestion to [SPECIFIC ACTION THEY RECOMMENDED]. If there's ever anything I can do to return the favor, please don't hesitate to reach out.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Thank You 2: After a Referral

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Quick note to say thank you for [REFERRING ME / MAKING THE INTRODUCTION TO NAME]. I really appreciate you putting your reputation behind me. I'll make sure to represent myself well and keep you posted on how it goes.

Is there anything I can help you with in return?

Gratefully,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Thank You 3: After a Formal Interview

```
Hi [INTERVIEWER NAME],

Thank you for taking the time to interview me for the [ROLE TITLE] position today. I enjoyed learning more about [SPECIFIC PROJECT/TEAM/CHALLENGE DISCUSSED].

Our conversation reinforced my excitement about this opportunity. In particular, [SOMETHING SPECIFIC ABOUT THE ROLE OR TEAM THAT EXCITES YOU]. I believe my experience with [RELEVANT SKILL/PROJECT] would allow me to contribute meaningfully to [SPECIFIC GOAL THEY MENTIONED].

I look forward to hearing about next steps. Please don't hesitate to reach out if you need any additional information.

Best regards,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Thank You 4: After Being Rejected

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

Thank you for letting me know about the decision on the [ROLE TITLE] position. While I'm disappointed, I appreciate the transparency and the time your team invested in the process.

If anything changes or if a similar role opens up in the future, I'd welcome the chance to reconnect. I remain very interested in [COMPANY NAME].

Wishing the team all the best.
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Thank You 5: After Getting Help from a Stranger

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I know we don't know each other well, so I want to say thank you for [WHAT THEY DID: responding to my cold email, accepting my connection request, taking the time to chat]. It means a lot during a job search when someone you don't know takes the time to help.

[ONE SPECIFIC THING YOU LEARNED OR APPRECIATED FROM THE INTERACTION.]

I hope to return the favor someday. Please don't hesitate to reach out if I can ever be useful to you.

[YOUR NAME]
```

## Rules of Cold Outreach

1. **Personalize every message.** If you could send the same message to 100 people, it is too generic.
2. **Keep it short.** Under 150 words for emails. Under 300 characters for LinkedIn notes.
3. **Make the ask small.** "15 minutes" not "pick your brain." "Quick question" not "I'd love to learn everything about your career."
4. **Follow up, but don't stalk.** Three touches maximum. Then move on.
5. **Always give before you take.** Share an article, offer a perspective, congratulate them on something.
6. **Send on Tuesday through Thursday.** Response rates drop on Mondays and Fridays.
7. **Morning sends outperform afternoon.** 8-10 AM in their time zone.
8. **Track everything.** Use a simple spreadsheet: Name, Company, Date Sent, Follow-Up Date, Response.
9. **Expect a 15-25% response rate.** That means 75-85% of people will not respond. That is normal. Do not take it personally.
10. **Be grateful.** Anyone who responds to a cold message is doing you a favor. Act accordingly.

---

## Key Takeaway

Cold outreach is a numbers game wrapped in a quality game. You need volume (send enough messages) and quality (make each one personal and specific). These 30 scripts give you the quality. Your job is to provide the volume.

Customize a script. Hit send. Repeat.

# Follow-Up Scripts

**15 word-for-word scripts for following up at every stage of the job search. The fortune is in the follow-up.**

> **Disclaimer:** These scripts are starting points you can adapt. Response rates depend on timing, context, and the recipient. No specific outcome is guaranteed.

You don't need to send all of these today. Use the one that matches where you are right now.

**Time estimate:** 15 minutes to read, then reference as situations arise
**What you'll need:** Your application tracker, sent email records, and calendar

---

## Follow-Up Rules

1. **Follow up exactly once.** One follow-up per outreach or interaction. If they don't respond after your follow-up, close it and move on.
2. **Wait 5-7 business days** between your initial outreach and follow-up (unless they gave a specific timeline).
3. **If they gave a timeline** ("we'll be in touch by Friday"), wait 2 business days after their stated deadline before following up.
4. **Keep it shorter than your original message.** The follow-up is a nudge, not a repeat.
5. **Add something new** when possible. A new piece of information, a reference to something recent, or a simpler ask.

## Post-Application Follow-Ups

### Script 1: Application Follow-Up (No Response)

```
Subject: Following up — [ROLE TITLE] application

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I applied for the [ROLE TITLE] position on [DATE] and wanted to
follow up. I'm very interested in this role, particularly because
[ONE SPECIFIC REASON TIED TO THE COMPANY OR ROLE].

I bring [X YEARS] of experience in [FIELD] and believe I could
contribute meaningfully to [SPECIFIC TEAM OR INITIATIVE].

Is there any additional information I can provide to support my
application?

Thank you for your time.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 2: Application Follow-Up (After Referral)

```
Subject: Following up — [ROLE TITLE] (referred by [NAME])

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I applied for the [ROLE TITLE] on [DATE] after [REFERRER NAME]
encouraged me to look into it. I wanted to follow up and
reiterate my interest.

[REFERRER NAME] can speak to my work in [RELEVANT AREA]. I'd
welcome the chance to discuss how my background in [SPECIFIC
SKILL/EXPERIENCE] aligns with what you're looking for.

Happy to provide anything else that would be helpful.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## Post-Interview Follow-Ups

### Script 3: Thank-You Email (Send Within 24 Hours)

```
Subject: Thank you — [ROLE TITLE] conversation

Hi [INTERVIEWER NAME],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today about the
[ROLE TITLE]. I enjoyed learning about [SPECIFIC THING DISCUSSED:
project, team challenge, company direction].

Our conversation reinforced my interest, particularly
[SPECIFIC POINT THAT EXCITED YOU]. I'm confident my experience
in [RELEVANT AREA] would allow me to contribute quickly.

Please don't hesitate to reach out if you need anything else
from me. Looking forward to next steps.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 4: Thank-You Email (Panel Interview)

```
Subject: Thank you — [ROLE TITLE] panel interview

Hi [LEAD INTERVIEWER NAME],

Thank you to you and the team for today's conversation. I
appreciated hearing [SPECIFIC PERSPECTIVE FROM DIFFERENT PANEL
MEMBERS].

I was especially interested in [SPECIFIC CHALLENGE OR PROJECT
MENTIONED]. At [PREVIOUS COMPANY], I tackled something similar
by [BRIEF APPROACH], which resulted in [OUTCOME].

I'd love to bring that same approach to [COMPANY]. Looking
forward to hearing about next steps.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]

P.S. Would you mind passing along my thanks to [OTHER PANEL
MEMBER NAMES]?
```

### Script 5: Following Up When They Said "We'll Let You Know by [Date]"

```
Subject: Checking in — [ROLE TITLE]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I hope you're doing well. You mentioned I'd hear back by
[DATE THEY GAVE], and I wanted to check in.

I remain very interested in the [ROLE TITLE] and am happy to
provide any additional information or references. I understand
hiring timelines can shift, so no pressure at all.

Thanks for keeping me in the loop.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 6: Following Up After Final Round (No Response)

```
Subject: Following up — [ROLE TITLE] final round

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I wanted to follow up on our final-round conversation on [DATE].
I've been thinking about [SPECIFIC TOPIC FROM THE INTERVIEW] and
am increasingly excited about the opportunity.

I understand these decisions take time. Is there anything I can
provide to help move things forward? I'm happy to connect with
additional team members or share references at your convenience.

Looking forward to your update.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## Networking Follow-Ups

### Script 7: After an Informational Interview

```
Subject: Thank you for your time, [FIRST NAME]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

Thank you for the conversation today. Your insight on [SPECIFIC
TOPIC DISCUSSED] was genuinely helpful, especially [ONE SPECIFIC
TAKEAWAY].

I'm going to act on your suggestion to [SPECIFIC ADVICE THEY
GAVE]. I'll keep you posted on how it goes.

If I can ever return the favor or be helpful in any way, please
don't hesitate to reach out.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 8: After a Networking Event

```
Subject: Great meeting you at [EVENT]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

It was great connecting at [EVENT NAME] [YESTERDAY/ON DAY].
I enjoyed our conversation about [SPECIFIC TOPIC].

I'd love to stay in touch. [If relevant: You mentioned [SPECIFIC
THING], and I'd be happy to share [RESOURCE/CONNECTION/INSIGHT]
if that would be useful.]

Would you be open to connecting on LinkedIn as well?

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 9: Keeping a Contact Warm (Monthly Check-In)

```
Subject: Quick update + checking in

Hi [FIRST NAME],

Wanted to share a quick update: [ONE SENTENCE ABOUT YOUR SEARCH
PROGRESS OR SOMETHING RELEVANT TO THEM].

I also came across [ARTICLE/RESOURCE/NEWS] and thought of our
conversation about [TOPIC]. Figured you might find it interesting:
[LINK].

Hope things are going well on your end. Let me know if there's
anything I can do for you.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 10: Re-Engaging a Cold Contact

```
Subject: It's been a while — checking in

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I hope you're well. It's been [TIME PERIOD] since we last
connected, and I wanted to reach out.

I've been [BRIEF UPDATE ON YOUR SITUATION]. I remember you
mentioned [SOMETHING FROM YOUR PREVIOUS CONVERSATION], and
I've been thinking about that as I explore my next move.

Would you be open to a quick catch-up? I'd love to hear
what you've been up to as well.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

a quick update so I can plan accordingly.

Thanks for your time.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## The Break-Up Emails

Sometimes you need to close the loop yourself. These are for when you've followed up once and heard nothing.

### Script 13: The Graceful Close (Hiring Manager)

```
Subject: Closing the loop — [ROLE TITLE]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I've followed up a couple of times about the [ROLE TITLE] and
understand you may have moved forward with other candidates or
the role may have changed.

I'll close this loop on my end, but I want you to know I'd still
welcome a conversation if the timing is right in the future. My
contact info is below.

Wishing you and the team all the best.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 14: The Graceful Close (Recruiter)

```
Subject: Checking in one last time — [ROLE TITLE]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I wanted to reach out one final time about the [ROLE TITLE] at
[COMPANY]. If the role has been filled or the search has paused,
no hard feelings at all.

If something else comes across your desk that matches my
background in [FIELD/EXPERTISE], I'd love to be considered.
I'll keep my profile updated on [PLATFORM].

Thanks for your time throughout this process.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 15: The Bridge Burner (Just Kidding, Never Send This)

Don't write an angry follow-up. Don't tell them their process is unprofessional. Don't demand an explanation. Don't post about it on LinkedIn.

Instead, update your tracker to "Closed," take a deep breath, and apply to the next one. The energy you'd spend on frustration is better spent on your next opportunity.

## Follow-Up Timing Cheat Sheet

| Situation | Wait Time | Max Follow-Ups |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| After applying (no response) | 7-10 business days | 1 |
| After interview (thank you) | Same day or next morning | N/A (this is expected) |
| After interview (no decision yet) | 2 days past their stated timeline | 1 |
| After networking conversation | Same day or next morning | N/A (this is courtesy) |
| After recruiter call | 5 business days | 1 |
| After cold outreach (no response) | 5-7 business days | 1 |
| Keeping a contact warm | Every 3-4 weeks | Ongoing (with value added) |

---

## Key Takeaway

Following up is not desperate. It's professional. Most hiring processes are chaotic on the inside, and your message genuinely gets lost in the noise. One thoughtful follow-up often makes the difference between "forgotten" and "hired." Use these scripts, customize the [BRACKETS], send with confidence, and know when to close the loop. The follow-up is where most candidates drop off. That's exactly why it works for the ones who don't.

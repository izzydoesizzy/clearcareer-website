# Networking Scripts

**15 word-for-word scripts for informational interviews, coffee chats, referral requests, and LinkedIn engagement.**

> **Disclaimer:** These scripts are starting points you can adapt. Networking is relationship-building, not transactional outreach. Customize each script to reflect genuine interest in the other person.

You don't need to do everything today. Pick the script that matches your next interaction.

**Time estimate:** 15 minutes to read, then customize and use as needed
**What you'll need:** Your target company list, LinkedIn profile, and a list of people you'd like to connect with

---

## Networking Rules

1. **Give before you ask.** Share an article, make an introduction, offer genuine praise before you request anything.
2. **Be specific in your ask.** "Can you help me?" is vague and stressful for the other person. "Would you have 15 minutes to share your experience moving from consulting to in-house strategy?" is clear and easy to say yes to.
3. **Respect time limits.** If you asked for 15 minutes, end at 15 minutes. They can extend it if they want to. You don't.
4. **Follow up with value.** After every networking conversation, send a thank-you within 24 hours and reference something specific from the conversation.
5. **Don't ask for a job.** Ask for insight, advice, perspective, or a connection. If there's a job, they'll mention it.

## Informational Interview Requests

### Script 1: The Direct Request (Via Email)

```
Subject: 15-minute conversation request — [THEIR FIELD/COMPANY]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

My name is [YOUR NAME], and I'm a [YOUR ROLE/BACKGROUND]. I'm
exploring [TYPE OF ROLE] opportunities in [INDUSTRY] and your
career path caught my attention.

Specifically, I'm interested in how you [SPECIFIC ASPECT OF
THEIR CAREER: transitioned to this field / built expertise in X /
navigated the move from Y to Z].

Would you have 15 minutes for a brief call? I have a few focused
questions and would be grateful for your perspective.

Happy to work around your schedule.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 2: The Direct Request (Via LinkedIn DM)

```
Hi [FIRST NAME],

I'm a [YOUR ROLE] exploring opportunities in [FIELD/INDUSTRY].
Your path from [PREVIOUS ROLE] to [CURRENT ROLE] is exactly the
kind of transition I'm considering.

Would you have 15 minutes for a quick call? I have 2-3 specific
questions and would appreciate your perspective.

No pressure if the timing doesn't work. Thanks either way.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 3: During the Informational Interview (Opening)

```
"Thanks so much for making the time. I really appreciate it.

I'll keep this focused. I have three questions:

1. What does a typical day or week look like in your role?

2. What skills or experiences were most valuable in getting
   to where you are?

3. If you were in my position [BRIEF CONTEXT], what would you
   focus on?

And if there's anyone else you'd recommend I speak with, I'd
welcome an introduction."
```

### Script 4: During the Informational Interview (Closing)

```
"This has been incredibly helpful. Thank you for being so
generous with your time.

Two quick things before we wrap:

1. Is there anything I can do for you? I'm happy to return
   the favor in any way I can.

2. Is there anyone else you'd suggest I connect with as I
   explore this path?

I'll send you a follow-up email with [SOMETHING SPECIFIC YOU
PROMISED TO SHARE]. Thanks again."
```

## Coffee Chat Scripts

### Script 5: The Coffee Invite (Someone You Know)

```
"Hi [FIRST NAME],

It's been a while since we caught up. I'm in a career transition
right now and would love to pick your brain about [SPECIFIC
TOPIC]. You've always had great insight on [THEIR AREA OF
EXPERTISE].

Could I buy you a coffee [THIS WEEK/NEXT WEEK]? Happy to come
to your area. 30 minutes max, I promise.

Looking forward to it.

[YOUR NAME]"
```

### Script 6: The Coffee Invite (Someone You've Met Once)

```
"Hi [FIRST NAME],

We met briefly at [EVENT/THROUGH MUTUAL CONNECTION] and I
really enjoyed our conversation about [SPECIFIC TOPIC].

I'm currently exploring [TYPE OF OPPORTUNITIES] and your
perspective on [THEIR AREA OF EXPERTISE] would be valuable.
Could I buy you a coffee sometime in the next couple of weeks?

Completely understand if timing doesn't work. Appreciate
you considering it.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]"
```

### Script 7: Starting the Coffee Chat

```
"Thanks for meeting with me. I really appreciate it.

Before I jump in, how are things going with you? What's
keeping you busy these days?"

[Let them talk first. Show genuine interest. Then transition:]

"So here's where I'm at: I'm exploring [TYPE OF ROLE]
opportunities in [FIELD], and I value your perspective
because [SPECIFIC REASON]. I'd love to hear your thoughts
on [1-2 SPECIFIC QUESTIONS]."
```

## Referral Requests

### Script 8: Asking for a Referral (Direct)

```
"Hi [FIRST NAME],

I saw that [COMPANY] has an opening for [ROLE TITLE], and
I think it could be a great fit based on my experience in
[RELEVANT AREA].

I noticed you're connected to [PERSON / YOU WORK THERE].
Would you be comfortable submitting a referral or making an
introduction? I've already applied through the portal.

Here's a quick summary you can share:
"[YOUR NAME] is a [ROLE] with [X YEARS] of experience in
[FIELD]. Most recently, they [ONE KEY ACCOMPLISHMENT]. Their
email is [EMAIL]."

No pressure at all. I appreciate our connection regardless.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]"
```

### Script 9: Asking for a Referral (Soft)

```
"Hi [FIRST NAME],

I'm exploring opportunities at [COMPANY] and noticed you're
connected to a few people there. I don't want to put you in
an awkward position, so let me ask it this way: if you feel
comfortable pointing me toward the right person to talk to,
I'd be grateful.

And if it doesn't feel right, totally understand. Our
relationship matters more to me than any one opportunity.

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]"
```

### Script 10: After They Refer You

```
"Hi [FIRST NAME],

Just wanted to let you know: [CONTACT PERSON] reached out
and we had a great conversation. Thank you so much for the
introduction.

I'll keep you posted on how it goes. And please let me know
if there's ever anything I can do for you in return.

Genuinely appreciate it.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]"
```

## LinkedIn Engagement Scripts

### Script 11: Commenting on a Post (Thoughtful Engagement)

```
"Great point about [SPECIFIC ASPECT OF THEIR POST]. I've
experienced something similar in [YOUR CONTEXT]. What
worked for me was [BRIEF INSIGHT]. Thanks for sharing this."
```

Not: "Great post!" or "So true!" or just a clapping emoji. Those add nothing and build no relationship.

### Script 12: Responding to Someone's Career Update

```
"Congratulations, [FIRST NAME]! Well deserved. I've always
admired your work in [SPECIFIC AREA]. [COMPANY] is lucky
to have you. Wishing you all the best in the new role."
```

### Script 13: Engaging with Industry Content

```
"This aligns with what I've been seeing in [YOUR AREA OF
EXPERTISE]. The data on [SPECIFIC POINT FROM THEIR POST]
is especially interesting because [YOUR OBSERVATION OR
EXPERIENCE]. Would love to hear more about how this is
playing out at [THEIR COMPANY/IN THEIR WORK]."
```

## Reconnecting with Old Contacts

### Script 14: The Honest Reconnection

```
Subject: Reconnecting after too long

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I'll be honest: I should have stayed in touch better. Life
got busy, and I let too many good connections go quiet. You're
one of them.

I'm in a career transition right now and it's reminded me of
the people whose perspective I value most. You're on that list.

No ask here. Just wanted to reconnect and see how you're doing.
If you're up for a call or coffee sometime, I'd enjoy that.

Hope you're well.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 15: The Value-First Reconnection

```
Subject: Thought of you — [SPECIFIC REASON]

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I came across [ARTICLE/NEWS/RESOURCE] about [TOPIC RELEVANT
TO THEM] and immediately thought of you and your work in
[THEIR AREA].

Here's the link: [LINK]

I'm in a career transition right now and have been reconnecting
with people I respect in the industry. Would love to catch up
if you have a few minutes sometime.

Either way, hope the link is useful.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## The Networking Mindset

Networking feels transactional when you approach it as "I need something from you." It feels natural when you approach it as "I'm genuinely curious about your experience and want to build a real connection."

Before every networking interaction, remind yourself:

- This person was once where you are
- Most people want to help (it feels good to be asked for advice)
- You have value to offer too (your perspective, your network, your skills)
- One genuine conversation is worth more than 50 LinkedIn connections

---

## Key Takeaway

Networking is not schmoozing. It's building relationships with people who share your professional world. These scripts remove the awkwardness by giving you exact words for every situation. Customize the [BRACKETS], lead with genuine interest, keep your asks specific and small, and always follow up with gratitude. The best job opportunities come through people, not postings. These conversations are the real job search.

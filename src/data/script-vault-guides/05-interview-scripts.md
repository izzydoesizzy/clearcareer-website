# Interview Scripts

**15 word-for-word scripts for the questions that trip people up most, plus thank-you emails that actually work.**

> **Disclaimer:** These scripts are frameworks you should adapt to your own experience and voice. Rehearse them until they sound natural, not memorized. Interview success depends on many factors beyond scripting.

You don't need to memorize all 15. Focus on the ones you'll face in your next interview.

**Time estimate:** 20 minutes to read, then practice out loud
**What you'll need:** Your resume, 3-5 accomplishment stories, and a mirror or phone to practice with

---

## The Golden Rule of Interview Answers

Every answer should follow this structure: **Context, Action, Result.** What was the situation? What did you do? What happened because of what you did?

Keep answers under 2 minutes. If the interviewer wants more detail, they'll ask. Short and specific beats long and rambling every time.

## The Big Five Questions

### Script 1: "Tell Me About Yourself" (TMAY)

This is not your life story. It's a 60-90 second professional pitch.

```
"Sure. I'm a [YOUR ROLE/EXPERTISE] with [X YEARS] of experience
in [FIELD/INDUSTRY]. Most recently, I was at [COMPANY] where I
[ONE KEY ACCOMPLISHMENT WITH A MEASURABLE RESULT].

Before that, I spent [X YEARS] at [PREVIOUS COMPANY] focused on
[RELEVANT AREA], where I [SECOND ACCOMPLISHMENT OR SKILL].

What draws me to this role at [COMPANY] is [SPECIFIC REASON TIED
TO THE JOB DESCRIPTION OR COMPANY]. My experience in [RELEVANT
SKILL] directly applies to [CHALLENGE OR GOAL THEY'VE MENTIONED].

I'm excited to dig into the details today."
```

**Pro tip:** Your TMAY should be customized for every interview. The bones stay the same. The specifics (especially the last paragraph) should match the role.

### Script 2: "Why Are You Leaving / Why Did You Leave?"

For layoffs:

```
"The company went through a restructuring that affected
[X NUMBER] of positions across [DEPARTMENT/COMPANY]. My role
was eliminated as part of that change. It wasn't performance-
related. In fact, my last review was [POSITIVE DETAIL].

It gave me the opportunity to be more intentional about my
next move, and that's what led me here."
```

For voluntary departure:

```
"I've had a great experience at [COMPANY]. I've learned a lot
and contributed [SPECIFIC THING]. But I've reached a point where
I'm looking for [GROWTH OPPORTUNITY / NEW CHALLENGE / SPECIFIC
THING THIS ROLE OFFERS] that isn't available in my current
position. This role caught my eye because [SPECIFIC REASON]."
```

**Never:** Badmouth your former employer. Even if they deserve it. The interviewer will wonder what you'll say about them.

### Script 3: "Why This Company?"

```
"Three reasons.

First, [SPECIFIC THING ABOUT THEIR PRODUCT, MISSION, OR MARKET
POSITION]. I've been following [COMPANY]'s work on [SPECIFIC
INITIATIVE] and it aligns with what I care about professionally.

Second, the role itself. The focus on [KEY RESPONSIBILITY FROM
JOB DESCRIPTION] is exactly where my skills are strongest.

Third, the team. From what I've learned about [COMPANY]'s
culture [REFERENCE SOMETHING SPECIFIC: Glassdoor review, LinkedIn
post from an employee, something said in a previous interview],
it sounds like a place where I'd do my best work."
```

### Script 4: "What's Your Biggest Weakness?"

Do not say "I'm a perfectionist." Do not say "I work too hard." Give a real weakness with a real mitigation.

```
"I tend to [GENUINE WEAKNESS: take on too much, struggle with
delegation, get deep into details]. I've learned this about
myself and have built [SPECIFIC SYSTEM OR HABIT] to manage it.

For example, at [COMPANY], I noticed I was [SPECIFIC EXAMPLE
OF THE WEAKNESS SHOWING UP]. So I [SPECIFIC ACTION YOU TOOK],
which resulted in [POSITIVE OUTCOME].

It's still something I'm aware of, but I have systems in place
to catch it early."
```

### Script 5: "Where Do You See Yourself in 5 Years?"

```
"In five years, I want to be [REALISTIC PROGRESSION FROM THIS
ROLE]. Specifically, I'd like to have [DEVELOPED SKILL / LED
TEAM / BUILT PROGRAM / EXPANDED INTO AREA] that builds on the
work I'd be doing here.

What excites me about [COMPANY] is that I can see a path from
this role to [NEXT LEVEL], and I'm the kind of person who grows
with an organization rather than jumping every two years."
```

## Behavioral Questions

### Script 6: "Tell Me About a Time You Handled Conflict"

```
"At [COMPANY], I was working on [PROJECT] with [PERSON/TEAM]
and we disagreed on [SPECIFIC ISSUE].

I scheduled a one-on-one to understand their perspective. I
learned that their concern was [THEIR LEGITIMATE POINT]. I
shared my perspective, which was [YOUR POINT].

We agreed on [COMPROMISE OR SOLUTION], and the result was
[POSITIVE OUTCOME]. What I took away from that is [LESSON
ABOUT COLLABORATION OR COMMUNICATION]."
```

### Script 7: "Tell Me About a Time You Failed"

```
"At [COMPANY], I [SPECIFIC FAILURE: missed a deadline, made
a wrong call, underestimated scope].

The impact was [WHAT HAPPENED]. I took responsibility by
[SPECIFIC ACTION: communicated to stakeholders, proposed a
fix, adjusted the plan].

What I learned was [SPECIFIC LESSON]. Since then, I've
[SPECIFIC CHANGE IN BEHAVIOR OR PROCESS]. I actually think
that experience made me better at [RELEVANT SKILL]."
```

### Script 8: "Tell Me About a Time You Led a Project"

```
"I led [PROJECT NAME/DESCRIPTION] at [COMPANY]. The goal was
[SPECIFIC OBJECTIVE].

I [SPECIFIC ACTIONS: assembled the team, defined the scope,
managed the timeline, resolved blockers]. The biggest
challenge was [SPECIFIC OBSTACLE], which I addressed by
[YOUR SOLUTION].

The result was [MEASURABLE OUTCOME: delivered on time, under
budget, exceeded targets by X%]. The project was [RECOGNIZED
/ ADOPTED / SCALED]."
```

## Salary Deflection (During Interviews)

### Script 9: Early-Stage Salary Question

```
"I'm flexible on compensation at this stage. I'd rather
focus our conversation on the role and whether it's a mutual
fit. If we both agree this is the right opportunity, I'm
confident we'll find numbers that work.

Can you share what range you've budgeted for this position?"
```

### Script 10: Mid-Process Salary Question

```
"I've done my research and have a solid sense of the market
range for this type of role. I'm expecting something in the
range of $[LOW END OF MARKET] to $[HIGH END OF MARKET], but
I'm most interested in the total package and the opportunity
itself. What range have you had in mind?"
```

## Closing the Interview

### Script 11: Questions to Ask Them

Always have 2-3 questions ready. These show genuine interest and give you real information:

```
"What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?"

"What's the biggest challenge the team is facing right now?"

"How would you describe the team culture? What type of person
thrives here?"

"What's your favorite part about working at [COMPANY]?"

"What are the next steps in the process, and what's the
expected timeline?"
```

### Script 12: The Strong Close

```
"Thank you for the conversation. I want you to know that I'm
very interested in this role. Everything I've heard today
confirms that my experience in [SPECIFIC AREA] would allow me
to contribute quickly.

Is there anything about my background you'd like me to
elaborate on, or any concerns I can address?"
```

## Thank-You Emails

### Script 13: Standard Thank-You (Send Within 24 Hours)

```
Subject: Thank you — [ROLE TITLE] interview

Hi [INTERVIEWER NAME],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today about the
[ROLE TITLE]. I enjoyed learning about [SPECIFIC TOPIC FROM
THE INTERVIEW].

Our conversation reinforced my excitement about this role.
I'm particularly drawn to [SPECIFIC ASPECT], and I believe my
experience in [RELEVANT AREA] positions me to contribute from
day one.

Please don't hesitate if you need anything else from me.
Looking forward to next steps.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 14: Thank-You After Final Round

```
Subject: Thank you — [ROLE TITLE] final round

Hi [INTERVIEWER NAME],

Thank you for the final-round conversation today. Meeting
[ADDITIONAL PEOPLE MET] and discussing [SPECIFIC TOPIC IN
DETAIL] gave me an even clearer picture of the role and the
team.

I walked away more confident than ever that this is the right
fit. The [SPECIFIC PROJECT/CHALLENGE/INITIATIVE] you described
is exactly the kind of work I'm looking for, and my experience
with [RELEVANT SKILL/PROJECT] would translate directly.

I'm excited about the possibility of joining [COMPANY].
Whatever you need from me to move forward, I'm ready.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 15: Thank-You When You're Not Sure It Went Well

```
Subject: Thank you — [ROLE TITLE] conversation

Hi [INTERVIEWER NAME],

Thank you for meeting with me today. I appreciated the
candid conversation about [TOPIC].

I want to add something I didn't fully express during our
discussion: [POINT YOU WISH YOU'D MADE BETTER, BRIEFLY AND
CLEARLY]. My experience with [SPECIFIC EXAMPLE] is directly
relevant to the [CHALLENGE/GOAL] you mentioned.

I remain very interested in this opportunity and would welcome
the chance to discuss further.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## Pre-Interview Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Research the company (recent news, products, culture, competitors)
- [ ] Review the job description and map your experience to each requirement
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 accomplishment stories using Context, Action, Result
- [ ] Customize your TMAY for this specific role
- [ ] Prepare 3 questions to ask them
- [ ] Know your salary deflection script (don't wing it)
- [ ] Do a test run of your tech setup (for video interviews)
- [ ] Have water, notes, and a pen within reach
- [ ] Practice your answers out loud (not just in your head)

---

## Key Takeaway

Interviews are performances, and the best performers rehearse. These scripts give you the structure. Your job is to fill in the [BRACKETS] with your real experience, practice until the words feel natural, and show up with confidence that comes from preparation. You don't need to be perfect. You need to be prepared.

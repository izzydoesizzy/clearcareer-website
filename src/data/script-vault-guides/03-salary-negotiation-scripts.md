# Salary Negotiation Scripts

**15 word-for-word scripts for every negotiation scenario, from the first salary question to the walk-away.**

> **Disclaimer:** These scripts are starting points you can adapt. Negotiation outcomes depend on the employer, market conditions, and individual factors. No specific outcome is guaranteed.

You don't need to do everything today. Read it now so you're ready when the offer comes.

**Time estimate:** 20 minutes to read, then reference as needed
**What you'll need:** Market salary data for your target role (Glassdoor, Robert Half, Indeed, Hays, Payscale), your walk-away number, and your target number

---

## Before You Negotiate: Your Three Numbers

Every negotiation needs three numbers decided in advance:

1. **Walk-away number:** The minimum you'll accept. Below this, you decline.
2. **Target number:** What you actually want. This is what you ask for.
3. **Anchor number:** 10-15% above your target. This is where you start if forced to name a number first.

Write these down. Keep them visible during any compensation conversation.

## Deflection Scripts (When They Ask Your Salary Expectations)

### Script 1: The Early Deflection

```
"I'm open on compensation at this stage and more focused on
finding the right fit. I'd love to learn more about the role
before we discuss numbers. What range have you budgeted for
this position?"
```

### Script 2: The Research Redirect

```
"I've done market research and have a good sense of the range
for this type of role. But I'd rather hear what you've budgeted
first so I can tell you if we're in the same ballpark."
```

### Script 3: When They Won't Budge

```
"Based on my research for comparable roles in [CITY/MARKET],
the range is $[LOW END] to $[HIGH END]. I'd expect something
in that range, but I'm open to discussing the full package once
we're further along."
```

## Countering the Offer

### Script 4: The Enthusiastic Counter (By Email)

```
Subject: Re: Offer for [ROLE TITLE]

Hi [HIRING MANAGER],

Thank you for the offer. I'm genuinely excited about this role
and the team at [COMPANY].

I've reviewed the package and would like to discuss the base
salary. The offer of $[THEIR NUMBER] is below what I was
expecting based on market data for this role in [CITY]. Sources
including [GLASSDOOR/ROBERT HALF/INDEED] show a range of
$[RANGE LOW]-$[RANGE HIGH] for comparable positions.

Given my [X YEARS] of experience and my track record of
[SPECIFIC ACCOMPLISHMENT], I'd like to propose a base salary
of $[YOUR TARGET NUMBER].

I'm flexible and want to make this work for both sides. Would
you be available for a quick call to discuss?

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

### Script 5: The Enthusiastic Counter (By Phone)

```
"Thank you so much for the offer. I'm really excited about
this opportunity.

After reviewing everything, the area I'd like to discuss is
base salary. The offer of $[X] is a bit below what I was
expecting based on my research and experience. I was hoping
we could get closer to $[YOUR TARGET].

Given my background in [SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE] and the scope
of this role, I think that reflects fair market value. Is
there flexibility there?"
```

### Script 6: The Multiple-Item Counter

```
"Thank you for the offer. I'd like to discuss a few points
to make sure we're fully aligned:

First, base salary. Based on my research and experience, I'd
like to propose $[TARGET].

Second, [VACATION/SIGNING BONUS/EQUITY]. I currently have
[X WEEKS / $X BONUS STRUCTURE], and I'd like to maintain
something comparable.

I'm flexible on how we get there. What matters most to me is
that the total package reflects the value I'll bring."
```

## Beyond-Salary Negotiations

### Script 7: Signing Bonus (When Base Is Firm)

```
"I understand the base salary has constraints. Would a signing
bonus of $[5-10% OF BASE] be possible to bridge the gap?
I'm ready to accept if we can work that out."
```

### Script 8: Additional Vacation

```
"Vacation time is important to me for sustained performance.
I currently have [X WEEKS]. Could we match that, or would
an additional [1-2] weeks be possible?"
```

### Script 9: Remote Flexibility

```
"I've worked [REMOTELY/HYBRID] for [X YEARS] and am highly
productive in that setup. The posting mentioned [THEIR
ARRANGEMENT]. Would you be open to [YOUR PREFERRED
ARRANGEMENT], or at minimum [COMPROMISE ARRANGEMENT]?"
```

### Script 10: Performance Review Acceleration

```
"If the base salary is firm at $[THEIR NUMBER], could we
formalize a 6-month performance review with a defined path
to $[YOUR TARGET]? I'd be comfortable starting at the current
number with that structure in place."
```

### Script 11: Professional Development Budget

```
"I'm committed to continuous growth. Is there a professional
development budget I could access? Specifically, I'm
interested in [CERTIFICATION/CONFERENCE/COURSE], which would
directly benefit the work I'd be doing in this role."
```

## Handling Objections

### Script 12: "This Is Our Standard for This Level"

```
"I appreciate the context. My experience goes beyond the
typical profile at this level, specifically [RELEVANT
EXPERIENCE THAT EXCEEDS THE STANDARD]. I believe that
warrants an adjustment above the standard range."
```

### Script 13: "We Need to Be Fair to Other Team Members"

```
"I absolutely respect internal equity. I'm not asking for
anything outside market range. Based on my research, $[TARGET]
is competitive and fair. I'm confident the value I'll bring
justifies it."
```

### Script 14: "We Can't Go Any Higher"

```
"I understand there are constraints on base salary. Can we
explore other parts of the package? A signing bonus, additional
vacation time, or equity would help bridge the gap. I want to
find a way to make this work for both of us."
```

## The Walk-Away

### Script 15: Declining Gracefully

```
"Thank you for working with me on this. I've given it serious
thought, and unfortunately the total package is below what I
need to accept. This wasn't an easy decision, as I'm genuinely
impressed by [COMPANY] and the team.

If the budget opens up or the role evolves, I'd welcome a
future conversation. I wish you the best in finding the right
person."
```

**When to walk away:**

- The final offer is below your walk-away number
- They've been inflexible on every point (salary, benefits, flexibility)
- Your gut says no (trust it)
- You have other options that meet your needs

**When not to walk away:**

- You're walking away out of pride, not math
- The total package (not just salary) actually meets your needs
- You don't have other options and the offer is within 5% of your walk-away

## Negotiation Cheat Sheet

| Scenario | Your Move |
|----------|-----------|
| They ask salary expectations first | Deflect. Ask for their range (Scripts 1-3). |
| Offer is 5-10% below target | Counter with enthusiasm (Scripts 4-5). |
| Offer is 10-20% below target | Counter on multiple items (Script 6). |
| Base salary is firm | Negotiate signing bonus, vacation, equity (Scripts 7-11). |
| They push back | Use objection handlers (Scripts 12-14). |
| Everything is truly final and below your walk-away | Decline gracefully (Script 15). |
| You have a competing offer | Mention it honestly, never fabricate one. |

---

## Key Takeaway

Negotiation is not confrontation. It's a conversation about fair value. Employers expect you to negotiate. The ones who don't negotiate leave money, time off, and flexibility on the table. These scripts give you the exact words so you don't have to improvise under pressure. Decide your three numbers before the conversation starts, lead with enthusiasm, and remember: the worst they can say is no, and you're no worse off than before you asked.
